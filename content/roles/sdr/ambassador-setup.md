## Why This Matters

Before you make a single call or send a single DM, your online profiles need to look professional. Prospects will Google you. They'll check your LinkedIn. They'll look at your Facebook. If your profile picture is a selfie from 2019 and your headline says "Open to Work," you've already lost credibility before the conversation starts.

Obsidion provides you with branding assets — banners, email headers, email footers, and everything you need to look like a legitimate representative of the company. Use them. This section walks you through setting up each profile step by step.

## LinkedIn Setup

LinkedIn is your primary professional channel. Business owners check it. Decision makers are on it. Your profile needs to say "I help businesses grow" — not "I'm looking for a job."

### Step-by-Step

1. **Profile photo** — Professional headshot. Clean background. Good lighting. No sunglasses, no group photos, no logos. If you don't have one, take one with your phone against a plain wall in natural light. It doesn't need to be studio quality — it needs to look intentional.

2. **Banner image** — Upload the Obsidion-branded banner from your assets folder. This immediately signals you represent a real company.

3. **Headline** — Don't use your job title alone. Use a headline that speaks to what you do for people.
   - Good: `Helping local businesses get found online | Obsidion`
   - Good: `Connecting business owners with growth solutions | Obsidion`
   - Bad: `SDR at Obsidion`
   - Bad: `Sales Development Representative`

4. **About section** — Keep it short. 3–4 sentences max. Talk about what you help businesses with, not about yourself.
   - Example: *"I work with local business owners who want more customers finding them online. Whether it's a website that actually converts, showing up on Google when people search for what you do, or automating follow-up so leads don't slip through — that's what we build at Obsidion. If that sounds like something worth a conversation, I'm easy to reach."*

5. **Experience** — Add your Obsidion role. Title: "Sales Development Representative." Company: "Obsidion." Description: 1–2 lines about what you do. Link to the Obsidion website if available.

6. **Location** — Set to your market area. Prospects filter by location.

7. **Contact info** — Make sure your email and phone are visible (or at least your email). You want prospects to be able to reach you back.

## Facebook Setup

Facebook is where many small business owners spend time — especially in industries like food, salons, contractors, and local services. Your personal profile doesn't need to become a business page, but it should look professional enough that a business owner won't dismiss you.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Profile photo** — Same professional photo you used on LinkedIn. Keep it consistent across platforms.

2. **Cover photo** — Upload the Obsidion-branded banner. Same one as LinkedIn — consistency builds recognition.

3. **Bio / Intro** — Keep it simple.
   - Example: `Helping local businesses grow online with Obsidion`
   - Add your work info: "Works at Obsidion"

4. **Privacy settings** — Make sure your professional posts are visible to the public. You don't need to make everything public — but any posts about business, Obsidion, or professional content should be visible to people outside your friends list.

5. **Content approach** — You don't need to post daily. But occasionally sharing a relevant article, a business tip, or an Obsidion success story keeps your profile active and gives prospects something to see when they check you out.

## Email Signature Setup

Every email you send should have a professional signature. Obsidion provides a branded footer — use it.

### What Your Signature Should Include

- Your full name
- Your title: Sales Development Representative
- Obsidion (with the branded header/footer images)
- Your phone number
- Your email address
- A link to the Obsidion website (if available)

### How to Set It Up

1. Download the email signature assets from the Downloadable Assets section
2. Open your email client's signature settings
3. Paste the branded template and fill in your personal details
4. Send yourself a test email to make sure it renders correctly on both desktop and mobile

## Instagram Setup

If you're prospecting on Instagram (which you should be for lifestyle, food, and salon verticals), your profile needs to look like it belongs to someone in marketing, not a spam bot or a random teenager. Keep in mind that Instagram is more visual and lifestyle-oriented, so a mix of professional and appropriate personal content is fine.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Profile photo** — Use the same professional photo as LinkedIn and Facebook for consistency.
2. **Bio / Intro** — Keep it punchy. Say what you do, who you do it for, and tag the company.
   - Example: `Helping local businesses grow online 📈 | Marketing @obsidion`
3. **Link in Bio** — Include a link to the Obsidion website or your specific booking link if you have one.
4. **Highlights** — If you use Story Highlights, consider creating one for "Work" or "Business" where you can save tips, behind-the-scenes content, or Obsidion updates.
5. **Content approach** — You don't need a perfectly curated grid, but ensure your public posts are appropriate. If your account is purely personal and you prefer to keep it that way, consider creating a separate professional/creator account for outreach.
