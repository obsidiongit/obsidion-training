# Getting Paid

## How You Make Money

This role has a base salary plus bonuses. Unlike a commission-only role, you have a stable foundation. But the real money comes from the bonus structure — the more appointments you book and the more those appointments close, the more you earn.

Your total compensation has three parts:

| Component | What It Is |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Base salary** | $45,000/year — paid on a regular pay schedule regardless of performance |
| **Per-appointment bonus** | A bonus for every qualified appointment where the prospect shows up |
| **Close bonus** | A percentage of the deal value when an AE closes an appointment you booked |

---

## Base Salary

Your base salary is $45,000 per year. This is your floor — you earn this regardless of how many appointments you book.

It's designed to give you stability while you ramp up. But don't treat it as the goal. The goal is to earn well above your base through the bonus structure.

---

## Per-Appointment Bonus

Every time you book a qualified appointment and the prospect shows up, you earn a bonus.

**What counts as a qualified appointment:**

- You used BANT criteria to qualify the prospect (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- The prospect attended the meeting with the AE
- The prospect was a legitimate business owner / decision maker with a real need

**What doesn't count:**

- No-shows (the prospect didn't attend)
- Unqualified leads (not a decision maker, no business, no real need)
- Fake or duplicate bookings

## How to Maximize Your Earnings

Your bonus income depends on two things:

1. **Volume** — More qualified appointments = more per-appointment bonuses
2. **Quality** — Better-qualified appointments = higher close rates = more close bonuses

The SDRs who make the most money aren't just dialing more — they're qualifying better. A prospect who shows up, is the decision maker, has a real need, and is ready to act is worth far more than someone who "seemed interested."

### Things That Increase Your Bonus

- Booking with decision makers (not gatekeepers)
- Including detailed handoff notes so the AE walks in prepared
- Following the BANT framework consistently
- Sending appointment reminders to reduce no-shows
- Prioritizing hot and warm leads over cold volume

### Things That Cost You Money

- Booking unqualified prospects who waste the AE's time (they stop trusting your appointments)
- Not sending reminders (higher no-show rate)
- Sloppy notes that leave the AE blind on the call
- Chasing quantity without quality

## FAQ

**When do I get my first bonus check?**
On the pay cycle following your first qualified appointment that the prospect attends. Exact timing will be confirmed during onboarding.

**What if the prospect no-shows?**
No per-appointment bonus for that meeting. That's why appointment reminders and proper qualifying matter — they directly affect your paycheck.

**What if the AE doesn't close the deal?**
You still get the per-appointment bonus for any qualified appointment where the prospect shows up. The close bonus only applies when the deal actually closes.

**Is there a cap on bonuses?**
No. The more you book, the more you earn. There is no ceiling.

## The Bottom Line
