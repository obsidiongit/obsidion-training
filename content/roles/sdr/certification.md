# Readiness Check

---

## The Gate

You don't touch a live prospect until you've passed this gate.

This isn't complicated. The SDR role is about opening doors — not closing deals. But if you can't explain what Obsidion does, handle a basic objection, and navigate a cold call without freezing, you're not ready. Sending an unprepared SDR into outreach wastes leads that could have converted and damages the company's reputation before the AE ever gets a chance.

This certification is simple. Four gates. Complete them all to go live.

---

## The Four Gates

### Gate 1 — Complete All Training Sections

Work through every section in the SDR hub. Read them. Don't just click through.

- Your Role as an SDR
- Lead Lists & Accounts
- Ambassador Profile Setup
- Product Knowledge (Lite)
- Outreach Playbook (all sub-sections: phone, email, social, objections)
- Getting Paid
- Performance Expectations
- Daily Structure
- Meeting Cadence
- Accountability

**How to complete:** Mark each section as visited in the hub. No quiz needed — the assessment gates below confirm retention.

### Gate 2 — Ambassador Profiles Set Up

Your LinkedIn, Facebook, and email signature must be fully set up with Obsidion branding before you start outreach.

**What gets checked:**

- LinkedIn: Professional photo, Obsidion banner, updated headline, About section, Experience added
- Facebook: Professional photo, Obsidion banner, bio updated
- Email: Branded signature installed and tested

**How to complete:** Your manager reviews your profiles and confirms they meet the standard. If something is off, they'll tell you exactly what to fix.

### Gate 3 — Mock Cold Call

A live or recorded role-play where you run a cold call with your manager playing the prospect. This is not a full demo or closing call — it's the SDR version: open, qualify, handle one objection, and book the appointment.

**What you'll be evaluated on:**

| Category | What We're Looking For | Points |
|----------|----------------------|--------|
| **Opening** | Confident, clear, asks a question that gets them talking | 25 |
| **Qualifying** | Uncovers at least 2 BANT signals through conversation | 25 |
| **Objection Handling** | Handles one pushback without folding or arguing | 25 |
| **Booking** | Asks for the appointment with a specific time | 25 |
| **Total** | | **100** |

**Passing threshold:** 70 points

**Re-attempts:** Unlimited. Review the scripts, practice, and schedule another one with your manager on Discord.

### Gate 4 — Manager Sign-Off

Your manager confirms that you're ready to go live based on:

- All training sections completed
- Profiles are set up and professional
- Mock call passed
- You can explain what Obsidion does in plain language without a script
- You understand the BANT framework and can apply it in conversation
- You know how to log activity in the Outreach Dashboard

This is a judgment call, not a test. If your manager has concerns, they'll tell you what to work on. If you're ready, you go live.

---

## Re-Attempt Policy

There is no penalty for re-attempting Gate 3 (the mock call). Review the material, practice with a teammate, and try again. The goal is to make you ready — not to fail you.

---

## What Happens When You Pass

You're certified. You go live.

Your manager signs off, your status is updated, and you start active outreach. You'll still be ramping (days 1–30 expectations apply), but you're cleared to work real leads and book real appointments.

**Timeline:** You should be certified within your first week. The training content is designed for 2–3 day reads, profiles take a day to set up, and the mock call can be done as soon as you feel ready. If certification takes longer than 7 business days, that's a conversation — not because the timeline is arbitrary, but because the material covers the basics and a week is enough time to know them.
