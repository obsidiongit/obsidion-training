# Meeting Cadence

---

## Same Team, Same Meetings

SDRs attend the same daily sales meeting as Account Executives. Every day at 10:00 AM Eastern, on Discord. If you're an active SDR, you're on the call.

These meetings are where the team aligns. You'll hear what AEs are seeing on their calls, what's closing, and what's not. That information makes you better at your job — because when you know what a good appointment looks like from the AE's perspective, you start qualifying differently.

---

## Meeting Format

### Schedule

Daily at 10:00 AM Eastern. Put it in your calendar. Protect it.

### Platform

**Discord voice/video.** Be in the channel on time. Camera encouraged, not required. Mic required — you're expected to participate.

### Duration

Tight and structured. Time-boxed by leadership.

---

## Meeting Structure

| Segment | Time | What Happens |
|---------|------|--------------|
| **Opening** | 0–2 min | Leadership sets the agenda. Company updates, product news, announcements. |
| **Pipeline Round-Robin** | 2–18 min | AEs give deal updates. SDRs give appointment updates: how many booked, show rates, quality feedback from AEs. |
| **Wins & Lessons** | 18–23 min | What worked since last meeting? What didn't? One win, one lesson — keep it tight. |
| **Coaching / Focus Topic** | 23–28 min | Leadership covers one area: objection handling, talk tracks, process changes, product updates. |
| **Close** | 28–30 min | Action items, reminders, next meeting confirmation. |

## What You Bring as an SDR

Your update is different from an AE's. You're not reporting on deals and revenue — you're reporting on appointments and activity.

### Pre-Meeting Checklist

Before the meeting starts, have these ready:

- **Your numbers.** Appointments booked since yesterday. Show rate. No-shows. How many were qualified.
- **AE feedback.** Did any AEs flag a quality issue with an appointment you booked? Own it and explain what you'll adjust.
- **Your top prospect.** One lead you're working that looks promising. What's the situation, what channel are you using, what's the next step.
- **One ask.** Need help with a talk track? Stuck on an objection? Want feedback on your cold call opener? Bring it. These meetings are the fastest path to getting better.
- **One win or one lesson.** A script that worked, a channel that's producing, or an objection you finally figured out how to handle. Share it — the team benefits.

### What Not to Bring

- Vague updates. "Things are going well" is not a report. Numbers.
- Excuses. If the numbers are off, own it and say what you're changing.
- Questions you could have asked on Discord. Save the meeting for things that benefit from live conversation.

## Why SDRs Attend the Full Meeting

You might wonder why you're in a meeting where AEs are talking about deals and close rates. Here's why:

- **You learn what a good appointment looks like.** When an AE says "that appointment was great, they were ready to buy," you learn what qualifying looks like when it's done well.
- **You learn what a bad one looks like.** When an AE says "they had no idea why they were on the call," you learn what to fix.
- **You build team relationships.** SDRs and AEs work together. The AE who trusts your appointments will give you better feedback and help you improve.
- **You hear product updates.** Changes to pricing, new features, new verticals — all of this affects how you pitch and qualify.
- **You stay aligned.** In a remote team, these meetings prevent drift. They keep everyone on the same page.

## Attendance Policy

### Expectations

- Attendance is mandatory unless you've communicated to leadership ahead of time.
- "Ahead of time" means before the meeting — not during or after.
- If a prospect callback conflicts, take the call and tell your manager. Get a recap afterward.

### What Happens If You Miss

- **First absence without notice:** Noted. Expect it to come up in your next check-in.
- **Repeat absences:** Treated as a disengagement signal. In a remote role, not showing up is the same as not being here.

No formal punishment system. But leadership notices. Your teammates notice. Don't be the person who doesn't show up.

---

## The Point

Every day, 10:00 AM Eastern. Show up. Bring your numbers. Participate. It's the easiest way to stay sharp, stay aligned, and stay connected to the team.
