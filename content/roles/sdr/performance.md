# Performance Expectations

---

## What This Role Demands

You have a base salary. That's the safety net. But the base exists to give you runway — not permission to coast. The SDRs who treat this like a salaried desk job will earn $45K and wonder why they're not making more. The SDRs who treat it like a performance role will earn $65K–$85K+ and build a path to an AE seat.

The numbers below are your targets. They're built around one core metric: **qualified appointments booked per day.**

---

## The Core Metric

| Level | Appointments / Day | What It Means |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Minimum** | 1 | You're doing the job. Barely. This keeps you employed but won't make you real money. |
| **Solid** | 2 | You're performing. Bonuses are meaningful. Pipeline is healthy. |
| **Strong** | 3 | This is the target. You're a reliable producer and your comp reflects it. |
| **High Performer** | 4–6 | You're outperforming. Leadership notices. AE conversations start happening. |

**1–3 qualified appointments per day is the target range.** High performers consistently hit 4–6. If you're below 1 per day for more than a few weeks after ramp, something needs to change — either your activity, your qualifying, or your approach.

| **Appointments booked** | 5 | 10 | 15 |

| **Show rate** | 70%+ | 80%+ | 85%+ |

### Monthly Targets

| Metric | Minimum | Solid | Strong |
|--------|---------|-------|--------|
| **Appointments booked** | 22 | 44 | 66 |
| **Appointments held** | 15 | 35 | 55+ |
| **AE close rate on your appointments** | Tracking | Tracking | Tracking |

The close rate isn't something you control directly, but it reflects the quality of your qualifying. If AEs are closing 30%+ of your appointments, you're qualifying well. If they're closing under 15%, something in your BANT process needs attention.

## Ramp Expectations — 30 / 60 / 90 Days

You're not expected to hit full production on day one. Here's what a realistic ramp looks like:

### Days 1–30: Learn the System, Start Outreach

- Complete all training sections (product knowledge, scripts, objection handling, tools)
- Set up your ambassador profiles (LinkedIn, Facebook, email signature)
- Start making outreach calls by the end of week 1
- Shadow AE calls when possible — see what a good appointment turns into
- **Goal: Book your first appointments. Target 1/day by end of month.**
- Focus on learning the rhythm, not perfecting every call

### Days 31–60: Build Consistency

- Full outreach activity every day — calls, emails, social
- You should be booking 1–2 appointments per day consistently
- Show rate is being tracked — send reminders, qualify properly
- Outreach Dashboard is current — daily activity logged, every appointment documented
- Start tracking which channels and talk tracks work best for you
- **Goal: Consistent 1–2 appointments/day with 70%+ show rate**

### Days 61–90: Full Production

- Hitting 2–3 appointments per day
- Your show rate is above 75%
- AEs are giving positive feedback on appointment quality
- You have a personal rhythm that works — you know your best call times, your best channels, your strongest openers
- Pipeline of callbacks and follow-ups is self-sustaining
- **Goal: Consistent 2–3 appointments/day. If you're still below 1/day at day 90, that's a conversation about fit.**

## What the Numbers Tell You

| Situation | What It Means | What to Do |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| High activity, low conversations | Your targeting or messaging needs work | Tighten your lead list, personalize your outreach |
| High conversations, low bookings | Your qualifying or booking ask needs work | Review your scripts and objection handling |
| High bookings, high no-shows | Your qualifying is loose or reminders aren't going out | Tighten BANT, send day-before reminders |
| High bookings, low AE close rate | Appointments aren't qualified well enough | Review BANT criteria, get feedback from AEs |
| Low everything | Volume is the problem | More activity across all channels. Period. |

---

## The Bottom Line

1–3 qualified appointments per day. That's the job. Everything else — calls, emails, social, the Outreach Dashboard — is the engine that gets you there. Track your inputs, hit your targets, and the bonuses follow.

If the numbers aren't there, don't wait for someone to tell you. Check your activity, check your approach, and bring it to your manager. The SDRs who fix problems early are the ones who make real money.
