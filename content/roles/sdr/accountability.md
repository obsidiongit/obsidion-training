# What Wins Here & What Gets You Fired

---

## No Gray Areas

You have a base salary. That's more than the AE role offers. But having a safety net doesn't mean the standards are softer. The expectations are clear: book qualified appointments, maintain your activity, and be someone the team can count on. If you're doing that, you'll make good money and have a clear path forward. If you're not, the conversation will happen sooner than you think.

This section exists so there's zero confusion about what "doing the job" looks like.

---

## What Success Looks Like

### Activity & Output

- **Hitting your daily appointment target.** 1–3 qualified appointments per day is the standard. Not a stretch goal — the standard. The SDRs making $75K+ treat it as the starting point.
- **Consistent outreach activity.** Calls, emails, and social touches — every day. Not just on days you feel like it. Not just when the list looks good. Every day.
- **High show rate.** Booking appointments is half the job. Making sure the prospect actually shows up is the other half. Send reminders, qualify properly, and confirm the day before.
- **Self-sourcing leads.** Don't wait for the company to hand you every lead. The best SDRs build their own pipeline through LinkedIn, Facebook, referrals, and local research.

### Process & Discipline

- **CRM is always current.** Every call logged. Every email noted. Every appointment documented with full handoff notes. If it's not in the system, it didn't happen.
- **Handoff notes are detailed.** The AE should be able to walk into the meeting knowing who the prospect is, what their business does, what pain they expressed, and which products map to that pain. "Seemed interested" is not a handoff — it's a guess.
- **Appointment reminders go out.** Every time. No exceptions. A no-show that could have been prevented by a 30-second email is a failure of process, not luck.

### Mindset & Coachability

- **Implementing feedback quickly.** When leadership or your manager points out a pattern — a script that's not landing, a qualifying step you're skipping, a channel you're ignoring — you adjust. Not "eventually." Next call.
- **Bringing real data to meetings.** What's working, what's not, what objections you're hearing, which verticals are responding. The SDRs who share useful intel are the ones who get the most coaching back.
- **Owning your numbers.** Know your daily stats without looking them up. Know your show rate. Know which leads are hot. If someone asks you how your week is going, your answer should be numbers — not "pretty good."

## What Gets You Exited

### Activity Failures

- **Consistently falling below 1 appointment per day** after the ramp period. If you can't book a single meeting in a day, regularly, the math doesn't work — for you or for the company.
- **Going dark on activity.** Days with no calls, no emails, no social touches. In a remote role, silence is indistinguishable from absence. If you're not showing activity, we have to assume there is none.
- **Relying entirely on assigned leads.** If your only outreach is the list you're handed and you never self-source, your pipeline is fragile and your ceiling is low.

### Integrity Failures

- **Booking fake or inflated appointments.** If you're putting meetings on the calendar that you know aren't qualified just to hit a number, the relationship ends. AEs will flag it. Leadership will notice. There's no version of this that gets a second chance.
- **Misrepresenting Obsidion to prospects.** Making promises we can't keep, inventing features, or exaggerating results to get a booking. Your job is to open the door honestly — the AE handles the pitch.
- **Neglecting the CRM.** Empty records, stale data, missing notes. The CRM is how leadership sees your work. If it's blank, your work is invisible — and invisible work doesn't get credit.

### Engagement Failures

- **Missing meetings without communication.** The team meets twice a week. If you're consistently absent without notice, it signals disengagement. In a remote role, disengagement is the first step toward exit.
- **Not responding to team communication.** Messages, pings, follow-ups from AEs or leadership. If you're hard to reach during working hours, that's a problem.

### Development Failures

- **Refusing coaching.** You don't have to agree with every note. You do have to try it, test it, and report back. If the same feedback shows up three times with no change, that's a fit question — not a coaching question.
- **No improvement trajectory after ramp.** The ramp period has specific milestones. If you're at day 90 and still below minimum targets with no upward trend, the conversation shifts from coaching to whether this role is the right match.

## The Bright Line Framework

Use this to check yourself before leadership has to.

### Green — Thriving

Appointment target hit daily. Activity is consistent. CRM is clean. Show rate is high. AEs are closing your appointments. You bring useful info to meetings and adjust based on feedback.

### Yellow — At Risk

Activity is dropping. Appointments are inconsistent. CRM is getting stale. Show rates are dipping. You're doing parts of the job well but the trend isn't right. Yellow is a signal — fix it before someone has to tell you.

### Red — Exit Track

Multiple areas are failing. Activity is low. Appointments are rare or unqualified. CRM is empty. Coaching hasn't produced change. Red means the trajectory is headed toward exit. You can pull out of it, but it requires a sharp, visible change in a short window.

## 30/60/90-Day Checkpoints

### Day 30 — Foundation Check

**What we're looking at:** Have you learned the system and started producing?

- All training sections completed
- Ambassador profiles set up
- CRM set up and actively used
- Outreach has started — calls, emails, social
- Booking at least a few appointments per week
- Attending team meetings and participating

**What we're not asking for yet:** Full production numbers. You're learning.

### Day 60 — Consistency Check

**What we're looking at:** Are you in the game?

- Booking 1–2 appointments per day consistently
- Show rate is above 70%
- CRM is current and accurate
- Outreach cadence is established — all three channels
- Feedback is being implemented

**What we're watching:** If activity is still inconsistent and appointments are sparse at day 60, the conversation changes.

### Day 90 — Production Check

**What we're looking at:** Are you a producer?

- Booking 2–3 appointments per day
- Show rate above 75%
- AE feedback on appointment quality is positive
- Self-sourcing at least some of your own leads
- No persistent issues with CRM, reminders, or handoff quality

**What happens at day 90 if you're not here:** An honest conversation about fit. Not a surprise — you'll have had check-ins at 30 and 60.

---

## The Bottom Line

You have a base salary that most sales roles don't offer. That's an advantage — use it to build without financial panic. But the base is not the destination. The SDRs who win here are the ones who treat the appointment target like their livelihood depends on it — because their bonus income does.

If something is off, say something early. The window to fix it is always bigger than it looks from inside the problem.
