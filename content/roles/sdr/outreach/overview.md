# Outreach Playbook

This is your playbook for reaching prospects and booking appointments. You have three channels — phone, email, and social. You'll use all three, every day. Each one has a specific role in the process, and they work best when you use them together.

## The Three Channels

### Phone — Your Primary Weapon

Phone is how you book the most appointments. It's direct, it's personal, and it forces a real-time conversation. Most prospects won't respond to an email or a DM — but a phone call is hard to ignore.

**Use it for:** First contact, follow-ups, callbacks, and any time you need an answer now.

### Email — Your Follow-Up Engine

Email keeps the conversation going after a call. It's also how you reach people who don't pick up. A good email sequence after a missed call can turn a no-answer into a booked meeting days later.

**Use it for:** Post-call follow-ups, first-touch outreach when you don't have a phone number, drip sequences, and sending meeting confirmations.

### Social — Your Trust Builder

LinkedIn and Facebook let you warm up a prospect before you ever call them. A profile view, a connection request, or a comment on their post puts your name in front of them. When you do call, you're not a total stranger.

**Use it for:** Pre-call research, connection building, DM outreach, and staying visible to prospects who aren't ready to book yet.

## How They Work Together

The best SDRs don't rely on one channel. They layer all three in a sequence. Here's what a typical outreach cadence looks like:

### Day 1

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Call the prospect. If they answer, qualify and book. If not, leave a voicemail. |
| After the call | Send a short follow-up email (reference the voicemail). |
| Afternoon | View their LinkedIn profile. Send a connection request with a short note. |

### Day 3

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Call again. Different time of day than Day 1. |
| After the call | Send a second email — add a new angle or a relevant question. |

### Day 5

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Send a LinkedIn or Facebook DM — short, casual, reference your email or call. |
| Afternoon | Call one more time. |

### Day 7

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Send a "breakup" email — let them know you'll stop reaching out unless they want to connect. |

### After Day 7

If no response, move the lead to a long-term follow-up list. Circle back in 30–60 days with a fresh angle.

## The Rules

1. **Never rely on one channel.** A call + email + social touch is 3x more effective than three calls alone.
2. **Always reference prior touches.** "I left you a voicemail earlier" or "I sent you a note on LinkedIn" creates continuity. The prospect starts to recognize your name.
3. **Log every touch.** Call, email, DM — all of it goes in the CRM immediately. Not at end of day.
4. **Respect the "no."** If someone clearly says they're not interested, mark it and move on. Don't burn bridges by being pushy — today's no can be next quarter's yes if you leave on good terms.
5. **Speed wins.** When a lead comes in hot (inbound, referral, engagement signal), the first rep to respond wins. Aim for under 15 minutes on hot leads.

## Daily Outreach Flow — Quick View

| Block | What You're Doing |
|-------|-------------------|
| **First 30 min** | Check for new leads, claim inbounds, prioritize your call list |
| **Outbound Block 1** | Phone calls — hot and warm leads first, then cold |
| **Between calls** | Send follow-up emails for every call that didn't connect |
| **Midday** | Social touches — DMs, connection requests, profile engagement |
| **Outbound Block 2** | More phone calls — work the rest of your cold list |
| **End of day** | Log all activity, set callbacks for tomorrow, update lead statuses |
