# Phone Scripts

---

## How to Use These Scripts

These are starting points. Don't read them word for word — you'll sound like a robot. Read them, understand the logic behind each line, and then say it like a normal person. The structure matters more than the exact words.

Your goal on every call is simple: **get the prospect talking, qualify them, and book the appointment.** You are not closing a deal. You are not running a demo. You are getting 20 minutes on an AE's calendar.

---

## Cold Call Opener

You have about 10 seconds before the prospect decides if they're hanging up. Make them count.

### Version 1 — Lead With a Question

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion. Quick question — when someone Googles [their business type] in [their city], do you know where your business shows up?"

**Why this works:** It's a question they probably can't answer confidently. That gap is your opening.

**If they engage:** Move to qualifying questions (below).

**If they say "I'm busy":**

> "Totally fair — I'll be quick. Is there a better time today or tomorrow I can call back? I just have one question about your online setup and it'll take two minutes."

### Version 2 — Lead With a Problem

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion. I was looking at your business online and had a question about your website — do you have two minutes?"

**Why this works:** You're telling them you already looked at their business. That's more specific than a generic cold call.

**Before using this one:** Actually look at their website. Know what's wrong with it. If the site looks fine, don't use this version.

### Version 3 — Referral Opener

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion. [Referral name] mentioned you might be a good person to talk to — they said you've been thinking about [website / marketing / getting more customers online]. Do you have a couple minutes?"

**Why this works:** A referral is the strongest opener you'll ever have. Use this any time you have one.

## Qualifying Questions

Once they're talking, your job is to figure out if they're a fit. You're checking BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. You don't ask these directly — you uncover them through conversation.

### Need Questions

- "How are you getting most of your customers right now — word of mouth, Google, social, something else?"
- "Do you have a website? When's the last time it was updated?"
- "If someone searches for [what they do] in [their city], where do you show up?"
- "What happens when a lead calls you after hours — who picks that up?"

### Authority Questions

- "Are you the one who handles the marketing side of things, or is there someone else involved?"
- "If this turned out to be a fit, who would need to be part of that conversation?"

### Budget Signals

You don't ask "what's your budget?" on a cold call. Listen for signals instead:

- They're already spending on marketing (even if it's not working) = budget exists
- They have employees, a storefront, or multiple locations = established business
- They mention revenue, growth goals, or investments = money is in play
- They mention running Facebook or Instagram ads = budget exists **and** a potential pain point

**Useful question to surface ad spend:**
> "Are you running any paid ads right now — Facebook, Instagram, Google — or is it mostly organic?"

If they say yes: find out if it's working. If it's not, that's your hook for the Paid Ads service. If they say no: that's a growth conversation — they want more customers but haven't pulled the trigger on ads yet.

### Timeline Questions

- "Is this something you've been thinking about for a while, or did something change recently?"
- "Is there a timeline you're working toward — a busy season, a launch, anything like that?"

## Booking the Appointment

Once you've confirmed they have a need and they're at least somewhat interested, book the meeting. Don't oversell. Don't keep talking. Get the appointment.

### The Ask

> "Based on what you're telling me, I think it'd be worth 20 minutes with one of our specialists who can actually show you what this looks like for your business. I'm not the one who runs the demo — I just find the people it makes sense for. Can I grab a time on the calendar this week?"

**Why this works:** You're positioning yourself as the connector, not the salesperson. It takes pressure off both of you.

### If They Hesitate

> "No commitment — it's a 20-minute conversation. If it's not a fit, they'll tell you straight. I just don't want you to miss it if it is."

### Lock the Time

> "Does [specific day] at [specific time] work? I'll send you a calendar invite right after this call so it's locked in."

**Always suggest a specific time.** "Sometime this week" gets forgotten. "Tuesday at 2pm" gets booked.

## Voicemail Script

Keep it under 25 seconds. Don't pitch. Give one reason to call back.

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion — [your phone number]. I had a quick question about your business's online presence. Not a sales pitch — just one question. Give me a call back when you get a minute: [your phone number]. That's [Your name], [your phone number]."

Say your number at a normal pace. Repeat it once. That's it.

---

## Callback Script

When they call you back (or you're calling at a time they asked you to):

> "Hey [Name], thanks for getting back to me. Like I mentioned, I had a quick question — when someone searches for [their business type] in [their city], do you know where you show up on Google?"

Then move into your qualifying questions. Don't restart the whole pitch — they already know who you are.

## Gatekeeper Navigation

### When You Know the Owner's Name

> "Hey, is [Owner name] around? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."

If they ask what it's about:

> "I work with [their industry] businesses on their online setup — I just had a quick question for [Owner name]. Is he/she available?"

### When You Don't Know Who to Ask For

> "Quick question — who handles the marketing or website decisions there? I want to make sure I'm talking to the right person."

Once you have a name:

> "Great — is [Name] available? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."

**The rules:** Don't pitch the gatekeeper. Don't leave a long message with them. Get a name, get a transfer, or get a callback time.

---

## When the Call Goes Nowhere

Not every call is going to work. If the prospect isn't interested and you can't uncover any pain, exit cleanly:

> "I appreciate the time. If anything changes with your website or online marketing, feel free to reach out. I'll check back in down the road."

Log the outcome. Set a follow-up for 60–90 days. Move on.

## Call Flow — Quick Reference

| Step | What You Do | Time |
|------|-------------|------|
| **1. Open** | Name, company, one question | 10 sec |
| **2. Hook** | Get them responding to your question | 30 sec |
| **3. Qualify** | BANT questions — need, authority, signals | 2–3 min |
| **4. Book** | Suggest the appointment with a specific time | 30 sec |
| **5. Confirm** | Lock the calendar, confirm email for the invite | 30 sec |
| **Total** | | **~5 min** |

If a call is going longer than 5 minutes, you're either having a great conversation (qualify harder and book) or you're chatting instead of closing (wrap it up and book).
