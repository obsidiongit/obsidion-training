## Before you dial — Q&A

**TL;DR:** Obsidion is mostly paid ads (Facebook and Instagram) plus site, SEO, follow-up, app; you qualify and book — AEs scope deals.

**What this means for you:**
- Explain what we sell in English first; say "Meta" or "Facebook/Instagram" when you are tying to their setup.
- If they go deep on details you do not know, bridge to the specialist — never invent answers.

**What does Obsidion sell?**  
Usually **~80%** how we pitch: paid ads — **Facebook and Instagram** campaigns in the client's own ad account. The other **~20%**: website, SEO, remarketing, optional app — sometimes **site-only**, no ads yet. One team. You book a specialist; you do not quote the full stack on a cold call.

**What problems are we usually solving?**  
Often: ad spend without clarity, or locked out of their own ad account; scaling beyond referrals. Sometimes the loudest pain is a bad or missing website — still our deal. Your job is to hear what's loudest — not to lecture five products.

**What is my job on the phone?**  
Start a conversation, qualify (need, authority, budget *signals*, timeline), and book a short meeting for an Account Executive / specialist. You do not close deals, run demos, or quote pricing tiers.

**What should I avoid?**  
Sounding robotic, guessing pricing or technical details, long competitor debates, or staying past ~5 minutes without a booked meeting or firm callback. If you do not know something: say the specialist covers it in the meeting — then book the meeting.

## The call plan

Follow this sequence until it is muscle memory. Scripts below plug into steps 2–5.

1. **Prep (before you dial)** — Check Facebook/Instagram for content, boosts, sponsored posts. Skim site + GBP. One hook: ad activity, concrete site issue, or growth angle — not generic "Google rank" trivia.

2. **Open** — Your name, Obsidion, reason in under ten seconds. Do not pitch the full stack.

3. **Hook** — One gap: paid ads/spend, scaling, or something specific you saw. Avoid quiz-mode ("do you know where you rank?").

4. **Qualify (light BANT)** — Need, decision-makers, budget *signals*, timing. You're checking fit — not pitching every product. If you freeze, the **quick bridges** in the next section are ideas, not lines to read.

5. **Bridge to the meeting** — Short call with a specialist who can review their situation. You set the meeting; you do not run the deep dive.

6. **Lock time + confirm** — Specific slot. Confirm email for the invite. Repeat the time once.

## Quick bridges

You already have the six steps above. This is a **cheat sheet** — rough ways to move the call forward when you recognize what they're stuck on. Say it your own way; the meeting is where the real detail happens.

### Spend feels like a black box

*They might say:* paying someone; can't see the ad account; don't know if it's working.

*You could try:* "Totally get that — we run the ads in your own account so you can actually see where the money goes. Facebook and Instagram side. Our specialist can walk through it on a short call."

### Wants more than referrals

*They might say:* busy but want to grow; need more than word-of-mouth.

*You could try:* "Makes sense — a lot of owners hit that wall. We usually put real budget behind ads first, then make sure the site and follow-up aren't leaking it. I can get you with someone who maps that out."

### Hard to find on Google / Maps

*They might say:* don't know if we show up; competitors are everywhere online.

*You could try:* "Yeah, showing up locally matters — we usually connect that to what you're already doing with ads and your site. The specialist ties it together; I'm just trying to get you the right conversation."

### Site is embarrassing or missing

*They might say:* need a new site; embarrassed; DIY on Wix and it's not working.

*You could try:* "Fair — if people land on a rough page after an ad, that's wasted spend. We fix the site as part of the growth stuff, not a random redesign pitch."

### Leads go cold

*They might say:* people inquire and disappear; we're bad at following up.

*You could try:* "That's super common — we automate follow-up so stuff doesn't slip through, especially behind whatever's already bringing you leads."

## How to use the scripts below

Scripts are **structure + wording you can steal** — not a teleprompter. Pause after questions. Outcome: **conversation → fit → booked meeting or firm callback.**

---

## Cold call openers

~10 seconds. Lead with clarity — ads, scaling, or evidence — not a generic Google quiz.

### Version A — Paid ads first (default)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. I was looking at [business] on Facebook/Instagram before I dialed — are you running paid ads in-house, or is someone managing that for you? We help businesses run advertising with creatives and campaigns in your own ad account so you see the spend — that's on Meta, Facebook and Instagram."*

**Use when:** You see content, boosts, or sponsored posts — or the category is a natural fit for paid ads.

### Version B — Scaling (no clear ad signal)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. [Business] looks like it has traction — are you trying to scale what's working or is growth mostly referral-driven? We help owners put real budget behind paid advertising — usually on Meta, Facebook and Instagram — when they're ready."*

### Version C — Specific observation (you did prep)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. Before this call I noticed [specific thing — mobile, reviews, a post, booking flow]. Wanted two minutes — it ties into how we think about growth, usually starting with what you're doing on ads and what happens after someone clicks."*

**Rule:** Only if you actually looked.

### Version D — Referral

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. [Referral] suggested I reach out — thought you might be open to a conversation about scaling [leads / the business]. Got a minute?"*

**If they say "I'm busy":**

> *"Totally fair — two minutes. Better time today or tomorrow? I'm asking about how you're handling paid advertising online and whether you want help scaling."*

---

## Qualifying questions (conversational)

Ask one at a time. You are uncovering BANT without sounding like a checklist.

### Need & channel

- "Are you running any paid ads online right now — in-house, agency, or not yet? (If yes, Facebook/Instagram is usually where we start.)"
- "If you're spending, are you happy with how visible performance and spend are in your ad account?"
- "Where do you want the next chunk of growth to come from?"
- "When's the last time you looked at your site on your phone after clicking an ad or a post?"

### Authority

- "Are you usually the one on marketing and website decisions, or is someone else in the loop?"
- "If this looked worthwhile, who else would need to be on a short follow-up call?"

### Budget signals (listen; do not ask "what's your budget" cold)

- Mention of ad spend, agencies, or hiring
- Multiple locations, staff, or growth goals

**Useful follow-up:** *"Are you running paid ads or search right now?"* If yes → are they happy with it? That opens the ads/SEO conversation without you lecturing.

### Timeline

- "Is this something you've been thinking about for a while, or did something change recently?"
- "Any season or date behind it — busy season, reopening, new location?"

## Booking the meeting

### The ask

> *"Honestly, this sounds like a good fit — let me get you on a call with one of our guys who can look at your actual situation. You free [day] at [time]?"*

### If they hesitate

> *"No pressure — it's [15–20] minutes. If it's not a fit, they'll say so."*

**Lock a specific time.** "Tuesday at 2:00" beats "sometime this week."

---

## Voicemail

Under ~25 seconds. No product dump. Reason to call back + number twice.

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion — [number]. Wanted to ask how you're handling paid advertising online — we help businesses run ads with full visibility in their own account, including on Meta, Facebook and Instagram. Call me back: [number]. Again, [Your name], [number]."*

---

## When they call you back

> *"Hey [Name], thanks for getting back. Same topic — are you running paid ads yourself or with someone, and are you happy with what you're seeing in your ad account for spend and results? If that's fuzzy, that's usually why people talk to us."*

Then qualify → bridge → book. Do not restart a monologue.

---

## Gatekeeper navigation

**When you know the owner's name:** *"Hey, is [Owner] available? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."*  
If asked: *"It's about their paid advertising — I need two minutes with [Owner]."*

**When you don't know who to ask:** *"Who handles paid advertising or marketing spend? I want the right person."*

Don't pitch the gatekeeper. Get a name, transfer, or callback time.

---

## When the call goes nowhere

> *"Appreciate the time. I'll let you go — if anything changes, you know where to find us."*

Log it. Follow up in 60–90 days if your process allows. Move on.

## At a glance — call flow

| Step | What you do | ~Time |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **1. Open** | Name, company, reason | 10 sec |
| **2. Hook** | One question; get them talking | 30–60 sec |
| **3. Qualify** | BANT, conversational | 2–3 min |
| **4. Book** | Specific meeting time | 30 sec |
| **5. Confirm** | Email for invite | 30 sec |

**Total target: ~5 minutes** to booked meeting or firm callback.
