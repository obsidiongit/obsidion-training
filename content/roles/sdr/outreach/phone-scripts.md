# Phone Scripts

---

## Read this first

If you are new to Obsidion or to sales, **skim [Product Knowledge (Lite)](../product-knowledge-lite.md) first** — usually **≈80% Meta**, **~20%** rest of stack; website-only is still valid. This page is the call plan and scripts.

---

## Before you dial — Q&A

**What does Obsidion sell?**  
Usually **~80%** how we pitch: Meta (Facebook and Instagram) ad management — creatives and campaigns in the client's own ad account. The other **~20%**: website, SEO, remarketing, optional app — sometimes **site-only**, no ads yet. One team. You book a specialist; you do not quote the full stack on a cold call.

**What problems are we usually solving?**  
Often: spend on Meta without clarity, or locked out of their own ad account; scaling beyond referrals. Sometimes the loudest pain is a bad or missing website — still our deal. Your job is to hear what's loudest — not to lecture five products.

**What is my job on the phone?**  
Start a conversation, qualify (need, authority, budget *signals*, timeline), and book a short meeting for an Account Executive / specialist. You do not close deals, run demos, or quote pricing tiers.

**What should I avoid?**  
Sounding robotic, guessing pricing or technical details, long competitor debates, or staying past ~5 minutes without a booked meeting or firm callback. If you do not know something: say the specialist covers it in the meeting — then book the meeting.

## Pain you hear → how to bridge (without lecturing)

| Pain | They might say… | One-sentence bridge |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Meta spend blind / ads not performing | Paying someone; can't see the ad account / don't know if it works | "We run Meta in your account with creatives and management — full visibility. That's what the specialist unpacks first." |
| Want to scale | Busy but want more growth | "Most owners put fuel on demand with Meta — we pair site and follow-up so money doesn't leak." |
| Hard to find on Google / Maps | Competitors everywhere online | "Local visibility is part of the stack — tied to what you're doing on paid and on the site." |
| Website is old, slow, or missing | Bad site / DIY failed | "Bad pages waste ad traffic — we fix the site as part of growth, not as a random redesign pitch." |
| Leads go cold | Inquiries disappear | "Follow-up stacks behind the campaigns that drive the leads." |

## The call plan

Follow this sequence until it is muscle memory. Scripts below plug into steps 2–5.

1. **Prep (before you dial)** — Check Facebook/Instagram for content, boosts, sponsored posts. Skim site + GBP. One hook: ad activity, concrete site issue, or growth angle — not generic "Google rank" trivia.

2. **Open** — Your name, Obsidion, reason in under ten seconds. Do not pitch the full stack.

3. **Hook** — One gap: Meta/spend, scaling, or something specific you saw. Avoid quiz-mode ("do you know where you rank?").

4. **Qualify (light BANT)** — Need, decision-makers, budget *signals*, timing. Map what they say to the pain table — confirm fit, not features.

5. **Bridge to the meeting** — Short call with a specialist who can review their situation. You set the meeting; you do not run the deep dive.

6. **Lock time + confirm** — Specific slot. Confirm email for the invite. Repeat the time once.

## How to use the scripts below

Scripts are **structure + wording you can steal** — not a teleprompter. Pause after questions. Outcome: **conversation → fit → booked meeting or firm callback.**

---

## Cold call openers

~10 seconds. Lead with clarity — Meta, scaling, or evidence — not a generic Google quiz.

### Version A — Meta first (default)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. I was looking at [business] on Facebook/Instagram before I dialed — are you running paid ads in-house, or is someone managing that for you? We focus heavily on Meta: creatives and campaigns inside your ad account so you see the spend."*

**Use when:** You see content, boosts, or sponsored posts — or the category is a natural fit for paid social.

### Version B — Scaling (no clear ad signal)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. [Business] looks like it has traction — are you trying to scale what's working or is growth mostly referral-driven? We help owners put real budget behind demand on Meta when they're ready."*

### Version C — Specific observation (you did prep)

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. Before this call I noticed [specific thing — mobile, reviews, a post, booking flow]. Wanted two minutes — it ties into how we think about growth, usually starting with Meta and what happens after someone clicks."*

**Rule:** Only if you actually looked.

### Version D — Referral

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion. [Referral] suggested I reach out — thought you might be open to a conversation about scaling [leads / the business]. Got a minute?"*

**If they say "I'm busy":**

> *"Totally fair — two minutes. Better time today or tomorrow? I'm asking about Facebook/Instagram ads and whether you want help scaling."*

## Qualifying questions (conversational)

Ask one at a time. You are uncovering BANT without sounding like a checklist.

### Need & channel

- "Are you running Facebook or Instagram ads right now — in-house, agency, or not yet?"
- "If you're spending, are you happy with how visible performance and spend are in your ad account?"
- "Where do you want the next chunk of growth to come from?"
- "When's the last time you looked at your site on your phone after clicking an ad or a post?"

### Authority

- "Are you usually the one on marketing and website decisions, or is someone else in the loop?"
- "If this looked worthwhile, who else would need to be on a short follow-up call?"

### Budget signals (listen; do not ask "what's your budget" cold)

- Mention of ad spend, agencies, or hiring
- Multiple locations, staff, or growth goals

**Useful follow-up:** *"Are you running paid social or search right now?"* If yes → are they happy with it? That opens Meta/SEO without you lecturing.

### Timeline

- "Is this something you've been thinking about for a while, or did something change recently?"
- "Any season or date behind it — busy season, reopening, new location?"

---

## Booking the meeting

### The ask

> *"Based on what you're saying, I think it's worth a short call with one of our specialists — they can look at your setup and tell you what would actually move the needle. I'm not the person who does that deep dive; I set those up. Can I put you down for [day] at [time] this week?"*

### If they hesitate

> *"No commitment — it's [15–20] minutes. If it's not a fit, they'll say so. I just don't want you to miss it if it is."*

**Lock a specific time.** "Tuesday at 2:00" beats "sometime this week."

## Voicemail

Under ~25 seconds. No product dump. Reason to call back + number twice.

> *"Hey [Name], it's [Your name] with Obsidion — [number]. Wanted to ask how you're handling Facebook and Instagram ads — we help businesses run Meta with full visibility in their own account. Call me back: [number]. Again, [Your name], [number]."*

---

## When they call you back

> *"Hey [Name], thanks for getting back. Same topic — are you running Meta ads yourself or with someone, and are you happy with what you're seeing in your ad account for spend and results? If that's fuzzy, that's usually why people talk to us."*

Then qualify → bridge → book. Do not restart a monologue.

---

## Gatekeeper navigation

**When you know the owner's name:** *"Hey, is [Owner] available? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."*  
If asked: *"It's about their Facebook and Instagram ads — I need two minutes with [Owner]."*

**When you don't know who to ask:** *"Who handles paid social or marketing spend? I want the right person."*

Don't pitch the gatekeeper. Get a name, transfer, or callback time.

---

## When the call goes nowhere

> *"Appreciate the time. If anything shifts on Meta or growth, feel free to reach out. I'll leave it there for now."*

Log it. Follow up in 60–90 days if your process allows. Move on.

## At a glance — call flow

| Step | What you do | ~Time |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **1. Open** | Name, company, reason | 10 sec |
| **2. Hook** | One question; get them talking | 30–60 sec |
| **3. Qualify** | BANT, conversational | 2–3 min |
| **4. Book** | Specific meeting time | 30 sec |
| **5. Confirm** | Email for invite | 30 sec |

**Total target: ~5 minutes** to booked meeting or firm callback.
