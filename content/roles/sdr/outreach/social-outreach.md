# Social Outreach

---

## Why Social Matters

Phone opens doors. Email follows up. Social builds trust before either one happens.

**Voice:** **≈80%** lead with paid ads or growth when it fits; name **Meta** when you are specific. Otherwise a real hook (site, reviews, scaling). Human — no generic "how's your online presence?" DMs.

**TL;DR:** DMs should sound human: lead with paid ads or advertising; say Meta (Facebook/Instagram) when you explain how Obsidion executes.

**What this means for you:**
- Many owners do not know the word Meta — do not open with it in a connection note.
- Product Knowledge has the full service stack and wording; social is for short, credible hooks.

When you view someone's profile, comment on their post, or send a connection request, your name starts to register. Then when you call, you're not a total stranger — you're "that person from LinkedIn" or "the one who commented on my post." That small edge makes a real difference in connect rates.

Social outreach is not a replacement for phone and email. It's the layer that makes phone and email work better.

## LinkedIn Outreach

### Step 1 — Research Before You Reach Out

Before you send a connection request or DM, spend 60 seconds on their profile:

- What's their business? What do they do?
- Have they posted recently? About what?
- Do you have any mutual connections?
- Is there anything on their profile that tells you what they care about?

This takes a minute and makes your outreach 10x better than a generic message.

### Step 2 — Connection Request

Always add a note. A blank connection request looks lazy.

**Template — Standard:**

> "Hey [Name], I came across your business [or page / profile] — looks like you're doing great work in [their industry] in [their city]. Would love to connect."

**Template — After Viewing Their Content:**

> "Hey [Name], saw your post about [topic] — solid insight. I work with [their industry] businesses on paid advertising and growth. Happy to connect."

**Template — Mutual Connection:**

> "Hey [Name], noticed we're both connected to [mutual connection]. I work with local businesses on ads and growth — figured it was worth connecting."

Keep it under 300 characters (LinkedIn's limit for connection request notes). Don't pitch in the connection request. Just connect.

### Step 3 — The DM (After They Accept)

Wait at least a few hours after they accept before DMing. Don't pitch immediately — it feels transactional.

**Template — Soft Open (Ads-first):**

> "Hey [Name], thanks for connecting. I peeked at what [company] is doing on Facebook/Instagram — curious if you're running paid ads in-house or with someone, and if you're happy with what you see in your ad account. We focus a lot on advertising — Meta, Facebook and Instagram — at Obsidion. Open to a quick call if that's on your mind?"

**Template — Direct (If You Have a Reason):**

> "Hey [Name], I looked at your business after we connected. Noticed [specific observation — e.g. sponsored posts, site from ads, engagement]. We help [their industry] businesses scale with paid ads plus the site/follow-up behind it (we run that on Meta). Worth two minutes?"

**If they respond with interest:**

> "Awesome — the best next step would be a quick 20-minute call with one of our specialists who can show you exactly what we'd do for your business. What does your schedule look like this week?"

Book it. Don't keep chatting.

## Facebook Outreach

Facebook is the highest-volume prospecting platform for the verticals Obsidion serves — local services, food, salons, contractors, med spas. The average small business owner in these industries checks Facebook more often than their email. The platform has also evolved significantly: Reels now drive more reach than static posts, Groups are where the most engaged owners spend time, and the algorithm actively rewards fast engagement.

Your job isn't to understand Meta advertising deeply — that's what Obsidion does for clients. Your job is to show up on their radar, lead with a credible hook, and get the call.

### Reading a Facebook Page Before You Reach Out

Spend 60 seconds on their business page before you send anything. You're looking for signals:

**Strong signals (high intent):**
- They're posting Reels regularly but no "Sponsored" posts visible → active content, no paid amplification → hot hook
- They have good engagement (comments, shares) on organic posts → the content works, paid would scale it
- Their page looks professional but their reach seems capped → they've hit the ceiling of organic
- Bio mentions a website or lead form → they care about conversions, not just likes

**Medium signals:**
- Inconsistent posting (some good content, then nothing for weeks) → overwhelmed owner, not ignoring growth
- Lots of photos, no video → they haven't crossed into Reels yet → opportunity

**What to skip (for now):**
- No posts in 90+ days → hard to engage authentically, come back via phone first
- Only boosted posts with no organic presence → they may already have an ad relationship

### Finding Prospects on Facebook

- Search for local business pages in your target area and industry
- Join local business groups (Chamber of Commerce groups, industry groups, "businesses of [city]" groups)
- Look at who's posting in those groups — active posters are active owners
- Check the follower lists and "Likes" of well-known local businesses in their vertical
- Search "[industry] + [city]" in the Facebook search bar and filter to Pages

### Facebook Groups — The Underused Channel

Groups are the highest-engagement surface on Facebook right now. Business owners who participate in groups are self-selected: they're active, they care about their business, and they're more receptive to peer conversations than cold outreach.

**How to use groups effectively:**

1. Join the right ones — local business groups, Chamber groups, industry-specific groups for your verticals
2. Lurk for a few days before reaching out — understand the tone and who the regulars are
3. When an owner posts about a challenge related to growth, leads, or marketing, comment with something genuinely useful (not a pitch)
4. After a real interaction in the group, send a DM — you're no longer a stranger

**What not to do in groups:** Drop a pitch post. Post your company link. Comment "DM me for help with this." Group admins remove this and it kills your credibility with everyone watching.

### Engaging Before You DM

Before you send any DM on Facebook, leave a mark first:

- Like a recent post from their business page (from your personal profile or a professional one)
- Leave a real comment — "That [dish/product/project] looks great" or "Love the before/after on that one" — keep it specific, not generic
- If they posted a Reel, watch it fully and comment — Facebook's algorithm shows them who engaged with their video, and a comment on a Reel carries more weight than a comment on a static post right now

Do this 24–48 hours before the DM. When your message arrives, they've already seen your name.

### Facebook DM Templates

**Template — After Engaging With Their Reels/Content:**

> "Hey [Name], been following [their business] — you're putting out good content. Quick question: are you running any paid ads behind the videos, or is it all organic right now? I work with Obsidion on advertising — we run Facebook and Instagram ads for businesses like yours — and wanted to see if scaling this is something on your radar."

**Template — Strong Organic Content, No Ads:**

> "Hey [Name], saw [their business page] and noticed the content is solid but you're not running paid behind it. That's actually a good spot to be in — it usually means the creative's already there. We help businesses in [their vertical] put real budget behind what's working, run through their Meta ad account. Worth a quick chat?"

**Template — From a Business Group:**

> "Hey [Name], saw your post in [group] about [topic] — made sense. I help local businesses put paid behind what's already working, usually starting with Facebook and Instagram ads. Open to a quick chat if growth is on your mind?"

**Template — Direct:**

> "Hey [Name], came across [their business] — content's strong. Curious if you're trying to scale reach with paid ads or if it's been mostly organic. We run paid ads for businesses like yours through their Meta account — Facebook and Instagram. Happy to compare notes."

**Keep DMs under 150 words.** Facebook DMs are read on a phone. Long messages get skimmed or ignored. Lead with a real observation, ask one question, end there.

### Facebook DM Follow-Up

If they don't respond to your first DM, follow up once — at day 5 — with a new angle, not a bump. Maximum two DMs total before moving to another channel (phone, email). Don't send three messages to someone who's read and ignored the first one.

**Follow-up template:**

> "Hey [Name], following up on my last note. Noticed [new observation — a recent post, a new Reel, a seasonal promotion they ran]. Still happy to connect if the timing's better now."

## Instagram Outreach

Instagram is the most visual platform in your rotation and the most direct line to owners in food, salons, med spas, and lifestyle businesses. These owners post daily, check DMs more than email, and make decisions based on what they see. The platform has shifted heavily toward Reels — short vertical videos now drive 35–40% more reach than photos for business accounts, and the algorithm pushes them to new audiences automatically.

Your angle is simple: they're creating content that could be reaching 10x more people with paid behind it. Your job is to show you noticed, ask the right question, and get on the phone.

### Reading an Instagram Profile Before You Reach Out

Before you DM, check their profile for 60 seconds:

**Strong signals (high intent):**
- Posting Reels consistently, no "Sponsored" label on any posts → organic only, paid would scale it dramatically
- Strong engagement relative to followers (comments, shares, saves) → the content works, algorithmic amplification would accelerate it
- Bio has a link to a website or booking page → they care about conversion, not just followers
- Aesthetic is tight, product/service is photogenic → the creative is already there

**Medium signals:**
- Mix of Reels and photos, some engagement, no clear ad strategy → standard local business, not optimized
- Good follower count, low engagement → they grew organically but aren't converting the audience

**Note for Reels specifically:** If their Reels are getting strong organic views (you can see view counts), that means the content is already working — paid would compound it. If their Reels are getting low views despite solid content, that's a sign they need reach behind them. Both are hooks.

### Finding Prospects on Instagram

- Search hashtags combining their industry and city: `#austinrestaurant`, `#dallasmassage`, `#denversalon`, `#phoenixcontractor`
- Check the Explore page in your area — Instagram's algorithm surfaces active local businesses
- Look at who's tagged in location-based posts for their city and vertical
- Browse the follower list of a well-known local competitor or anchor business in their vertical
- Search the business name directly if you're working a list — most have an Instagram presence even if it's not linked anywhere

### Engagement Before Outreach

The rule on Instagram is the same as Facebook: put your name on their radar before the DM hits. Instagram-specific:

- Watch their most recent Reel in full — this registers in their account insights
- Leave a specific comment on a Reel (not a photo — Reels get more algorithmic weight right now): "That [dish/service/result] came out great" or "The before/after on this is wild"
- Reply to one of their Stories if they have one up — Story replies go directly to DMs, making it the warmest possible entry into a conversation

Replying to a Story is the most natural DM entry on Instagram. It doesn't feel like outreach — it feels like a real person reacting to something they posted. Use it when the Story gives you a genuine hook.

### Instagram DM Templates

**Template — After Engaging With Their Reels:**

> "Hey [Name], your Reels have been great lately — the [specific one] performed really well. Quick question: are you running any paid ads behind the content, or is it all organic? I work with [their vertical] businesses at Obsidion on their Facebook and Instagram ads and wanted to see if it's something on your radar."

**Template — Strong Content, No Paid:**

> "Hey [Name], love what you're doing with [specific detail — dish, service, aesthetic]. The content's clearly working. Curious if you're trying to scale it with paid or keeping it organic for now — we help businesses like yours run ads through their Meta account (Facebook and Instagram) and I figured it was worth asking."

**Template — Direct:**

> "Hey [Name], came across [their business] here — content is strong. Curious if you're trying to scale reach with paid ads or if it's been mostly organic. We run paid ads for businesses like yours through their Meta account — Facebook and Instagram. Happy to compare notes."

**Template — Story Reply Entry:**

> [React genuinely to the Story first — "That looks incredible" or "The results on that are crazy"] → then follow up if they respond: "Glad it landed! Quick question — are you running any paid ads behind this content or mostly organic? That's actually what I do — help businesses like yours run Facebook and Instagram ads to get more eyes on what's already working."

**Keep Instagram DMs tight.** Under 100 words is ideal. Two or three sentences, a genuine observation, one question. If they reply, you're in a conversation — keep it natural and move toward the call.

### What the "Sponsored" Label Tells You

While you're researching, scroll their profile and the Explore page in their area. If you see a "Sponsored" tag on any of their posts:

- **They're already running ads** → the hook is "we manage and optimize in your account — have you been happy with what you're seeing in terms of results?"
- **They're DIY-ing it** → most small business owners who boost posts are leaving significant performance on the table vs. a managed Advantage+ campaign. That gap is your pitch.
- **They're using an agency** → ask about results and what they're paying. If they're not thrilled, there's a conversation.

No sponsored posts and strong content? That's a hot prospect. They've done the hard part (building an audience, creating content) and haven't turned on the engine yet.

### Instagram DM Follow-Up

Follow up once at day 5 if you don't hear back. Reference something new — a recent Reel, a Story they posted, a seasonal offer. Don't just send "following up on my last message." Show you're still paying attention.

Maximum two DMs before moving to phone or email. Instagram has limits on DMs from accounts that don't have mutual connections, and multiple unreplied messages looks like spam.

## Platform Intelligence — How to Use FB/IG as a Research Tool

Before any call or outreach, run a quick check on their Facebook and Instagram presence. This takes two minutes and gives you material that makes you sound like you've actually done your homework.

**What to look for on Facebook:**
- Last post date — if it's been three weeks, mention it as a hook ("looks like posting slowed down recently — a lot of business owners tell me that's when they start thinking about paid")
- Whether they're running any Sponsored posts visible via the "Ad Library" (facebook.com/ads/library — search their business name)
- Their page review rating — high ratings with low reach is a common gap
- Whether they have a "Book Now" or "Shop" button on their page — tells you they've set up for conversion

**What to look for on Instagram:**
- View counts on Reels — a Reel with 200 views on an account with 2,000 followers is underperforming; a Reel with 10,000 views is organic traction that paid would amplify
- Whether their link in bio goes to a real website or a Linktree with outdated links
- How frequently they post Stories vs. Reels vs. photos — tells you their content comfort zone
- Whether they use location tags and niche hashtags — owners who don't are missing organic discovery

**Meta Ad Library** (facebook.com/ads/library) is public — anyone can search any business and see if they're running active ads on Facebook or Instagram. Use this before every prospect research session. It takes 30 seconds and tells you immediately whether they have an active ad relationship or not.

## Social Engagement Habits

These are small habits that compound over time:

| Habit | Why It Works |
|-------|-------------|
| **View 10–15 prospect profiles per day** | They see the notification. Your name starts to register before the call. |
| **Comment on 3–5 posts per day — prioritize Reels** | Real comments, not "Great post!" — add something. Comments on Reels carry more algorithmic weight right now. |
| **Reply to 2–3 Stories per day** | Story replies go directly to DMs and feel like natural conversation, not outreach. |
| **Check the Meta Ad Library before every research session** | Know in 30 seconds whether they're running ads before you pick up the phone. |
| **Respond to DMs within a few hours** | If someone replies, the clock is ticking. Slow responses kill momentum. |
| **Share one relevant post or insight per week** | Position yourself as someone who knows paid advertising. An Obsidion result, an industry stat, a Meta performance benchmark. |

## What Not to Do on Social

- **Don't pitch in the connection request or cold DM.** Connect first, add value second, pitch on the call.
- **Don't spam DMs.** Two messages max if there's no reply, then move to another channel.
- **Don't be fake.** "Love your business!" when you clearly haven't looked at it is obvious. One specific detail proves you actually looked.
- **Don't post in Groups with a sales pitch.** Groups are communities. Sales posts get removed and kill your credibility.
- **Don't argue in comments.** Ever. You represent Obsidion now.
- **Don't mass-message.** Personalized outreach beats copy-paste blasts. The extra 60 seconds of research shows — and the reply rates prove it.
- **Don't over-follow-up on Instagram.** Two unreplied DMs is the hard limit. More than that triggers spam filters and damages your account.

Here's how social fits into your multi-channel outreach cadence:

| Day | Phone | Email | Social |
|-----|-------|-------|--------|
| **Day 1** | Call (leave voicemail if no answer) | Follow-up email after the call | View their FB/IG profile, check Meta Ad Library, engage with a Reel or Story |
| **Day 3** | Call again (different time of day) | Second email — new angle | Like or comment on a recent post; send connection request on LinkedIn |
| **Day 5** | Call one more time | — | Send a Facebook or Instagram DM (referencing something specific you saw) |
| **Day 7** | — | Breakup email | — |
| **Day 30+** | Re-engagement call | Re-engagement email | DM check-in referencing new content they've posted |
