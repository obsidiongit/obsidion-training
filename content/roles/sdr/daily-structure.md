# Daily Structure

---

## Your Day Has a Shape

You have a base salary, but your bonus income is built on activity. Nobody is watching you clock in. Nobody is going to remind you to make your calls. The structure below exists because the SDRs who book 2–3 appointments a day all run some version of it — and the ones who book zero have one thing in common: they wing it.

The blocks below are a framework. The times are flexible — shift them to fit your life. What's not flexible is the sequence: launch, outreach, follow-up, close out. Every block needs to happen, every day.

---

## Recommended Daily Time Blocks

### Morning Launch (first 30–45 min)

This is not email and coffee. This sets up your entire day.

| Task | What You're Doing | Why It Matters |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Check for new leads** | Check the Outreach Dashboard and review your leads. Look for overnight inbounds, referrals, or new assigned leads. | Hot leads go cold fast. The first SDR to respond wins. |
| **Prioritize your list** | Sort leads by priority: callbacks first, then hot, warm, cold. | Working in priority order means your best opportunities get your best energy. |
| **Review scheduled callbacks** | Check which prospects asked you to call back today. | A callback you forget is an appointment you lose. |
| **Check appointment confirmations** | Verify appointments you booked are still on. Send reminders if needed. | No-shows kill your numbers. A 30-second reminder email prevents them. |
| **Set your daily target** | Write down: "I'm booking ___ appointments today." | Clarity on the target focuses everything else. |

### Outbound Block 1 — Social & Content (next 1.5–2 hours)

This is your content and social block. Knock out your daily posts, group activity, and DMs first.

- Publish your daily posts: 1 Facebook, 1 LinkedIn, 1 Instagram
- Post in 3 Facebook groups relevant to your target verticals
- Do 10 Facebook group comments / interactions — add value, don't spam
- Send Facebook DMs (10) and Instagram DMs (10) to prospects
- Ask for 1 referral from an existing contact or recent conversation
- **Target: All social and content activity done by the end of this block**

### Midday Execution — Calls & Email (1.5–2 hours)

This is your direct outreach block. Calls, cold emails, and follow-ups.

| Activity | What You're Doing |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Call businesses** | Work through your list — 10 calls. Callbacks and hot leads first. |
| **Cold emails** | Send 10 cold emails — first-touch or follow-ups |
| **Appointment reminders** | Send day-before reminders for tomorrow's appointments |
| **Dashboard catch-up** | Update the Outreach Dashboard if you're behind. Not at end of day. |

### Outbound Block 2 — Upwork & Overflow (1–1.5 hours)

Upwork bids and any remaining activity from the day.

- Submit 5 Upwork bids on relevant projects
- Finish any remaining DMs, emails, or calls from your targets
- Follow up on warm leads — anyone who engaged with your posts or replied to a DM
- Log your activity

### End of Day Close-Out (final 30 min)

| Task | Non-Negotiable? |
|------|-----------------|
| **Update the Outreach Dashboard with your final numbers** | Yes. No stale records overnight. |
| **Log all activity** | Yes. Posts, DMs, emails, calls, bids, referrals, appointments booked. |
| **Review appointments booked today** | Yes. Did you hit your target? If not, why? |
| **Prep tomorrow's priority list** | Yes. Callbacks, hot leads, follow-ups — ready to go before you log off. |
| **Send any outstanding appointment confirmations** | Yes. Every appointment booked today should have a calendar invite sent. |

## The Daily Checklist

Use this every day. Reset it every morning.

- [ ] Checked for new / inbound leads
- [ ] Prioritized call list
- [ ] Reviewed scheduled callbacks
- [ ] Sent appointment reminders for tomorrow
- [ ] Completed Outbound Block 1 (social posts, group activity, DMs, referral ask)
- [ ] Sent cold emails (10)
- [ ] Called businesses (10)
- [ ] Completed Outbound Block 2 (Upwork bids, overflow)
- [ ] Logged all activity in the Outreach Dashboard
- [ ] Updated all lead statuses
- [ ] Prepped tomorrow's priority list
- [ ] Sent calendar invites for all new appointments

## What a Great Day Looks Like

| Time | Activity | Outcome |
|------|----------|---------|
| 8:00 AM | Morning launch — check leads, prioritize, prep | Ready to dial by 8:30 |
| 8:30–10:30 AM | Outbound Block 1 — posts, group activity, DMs, referral ask | Social and content done, DMs sent |
| 10:30–12:30 PM | Midday — calls, cold emails, reminders, Dashboard | Calls made, emails sent, follow-ups done |
| 12:30–1:00 PM | Lunch | Recharge |
| 1:00–2:30 PM | Outbound Block 2 — Upwork bids, overflow, warm follow-ups | Bids submitted, remaining targets hit |
| 3:00–3:30 PM | Close-out — log everything, prep tomorrow | Dashboard updated, tomorrow's list ready |

Total: 2–3 appointments booked, all activity logged, tomorrow is prepped. That's the job done well.

---

## What a Bad Day Looks Like

- Started the day checking email for 45 minutes instead of dialing
- Made 10 calls, got discouraged, switched to "research"
- Forgot to send appointment reminders — prospect no-showed
- Outreach Dashboard hasn't been updated
- No clear plan for tomorrow

The difference between a great day and a bad day isn't talent. It's structure.

---

## The Point

Your hours are flexible. Your output is not. Whether you start at 7 AM or 9 AM, the blocks above need to happen. The daily checklist tracks the individual tasks — use it every day. The SDRs who build this habit are the ones booking 2–3 appointments daily. The ones who wing it are the ones wondering why their bonus checks are small.
