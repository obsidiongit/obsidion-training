# What Wins Here & What Gets You Fired

---

## No Ambiguity

This is not the section where we sugarcoat things. You are in a commission-only sales role. There is no base salary buffer. There is no "we'll figure it out after 6 months." The rules are simple: do the job or don't. If you do the job, you make real money and you stay. If you don't, the relationship ends — naturally or explicitly.

This section exists so there is zero confusion about what "doing the job" means.

---

## What Success Looks Like

These are the behaviors and outcomes that define a top performer at Obsidion. Not aspirational traits — specific, observable actions.

### Activity & Output

- **Hitting or exceeding activity minimums.** Calls, demos, and proposals per week are non-negotiable floors, not soft goals. Top performers treat them as the starting point, not the finish line.
- **Self-sourcing pipeline.** Nobody is going to hand you a full book of business. Thriving AEs prospect aggressively — LinkedIn, cold outreach, referrals, warm lists. If your only source of deals is inbound, you're one slow week away from a dry pipeline.
- **Closing at or above quota by end of ramp.** The ramp period is protection, not permission to be unserious. Use the draw to stabilize while you build — not as a replacement for production.

### Process & Discipline

- **CRM hygiene.** Your pipeline is always current, accurate, and properly staged. Every deal has a next step. Every call has notes. Leadership uses your CRM data to forecast — stale records don't just hurt you, they hurt the team.
- **Showing up prepared to every meeting.** Numbers memorized, pipeline updated, top 3 deals ready to discuss. Coming to a pipeline meeting without your numbers is the sales equivalent of showing up to a demo without knowing the product.
- **Immediate post-call CRM updates.** Not "before end of day." Not "when you have a minute." Immediately after hanging up. The deals that fall through are almost always the ones where follow-up was vague and notes were stale.

### Mindset & Coachability

- **Implementing feedback quickly.** When leadership calls out a pattern — a talk track that isn't landing, a close you keep blowing, a sequence that's getting ignored — you adjust in the next rep, not "eventually."
- **Bringing real intel to meetings.** What's working, what isn't, what objections you're seeing, what's resonating. The AEs who contribute this kind of information are the ones who get the most coaching back.
- **Earning trust with prospects through product knowledge.** You know the products cold. You know pricing. You know the integrations. You don't make things up when you don't know — you say "let me get you the right answer" and you follow through.

## What Gets You Exited

These aren't gray areas. These are the behaviors that end the relationship — slowly or quickly, depending on how fast they compound.

### Activity Failures

- **Consistently falling short of minimum activity metrics.** If you're not making the calls, demos never happen. If demos never happen, the pipeline dries up. There's a threshold where missing targets consistently stops being a coaching conversation and becomes a question about fit.
- **Lack of urgency.** Slow follow-ups. Missed callbacks. Proposals sitting untouched for days. Letting deals die because you didn't nudge. Speed matters — the rep who responds in 10 minutes closes at 4x the rate of the one who waits until tomorrow.

### Integrity Failures

- **Misrepresenting the product, pricing, or terms to prospects.** If you're telling prospects things that aren't true to push a deal through — exaggerating results, inventing features, soft-pedaling cancellation terms — the relationship ends. There is no version of this that gets a second chance.
- **Stale or inaccurate CRM data.** If it's not in the system, it didn't happen. Leadership uses your pipeline to forecast real revenue. If you're leaving records empty or carrying phantom deals, you're making decisions based on fiction — and so is everyone above you.

### Engagement Failures

- **Missing meetings without communication.** One absence gets noted. A pattern of missing meetings signals disengagement — and in a remote, commission-only environment, disengagement is usually the first sign that the relationship is already ending in practice.
- **Showing up unprepared repeatedly.** To meetings, demos, and calls. Being the person who doesn't know their numbers, hasn't updated their CRM, and is winging it on a live prospect call is a performance issue — even when any single instance feels minor.

### Development Failures

- **Unwillingness to take coaching and adapt.** You don't have to agree with every note. You do have to implement it, test it, and report back on what you're seeing. If the same feedback surfaces three times with no visible change, that's not a coaching problem — it's a fit problem.
- **Missing quota after ramp with no clear improvement trajectory.** The ramp period has specific milestones. If you're approaching day 90 and the numbers aren't there — and the trend line isn't improving — the conversation shifts from performance coaching to whether this seat is the right match.

## The Bright Line Framework

Where do you stand right now? This isn't a formal review tool — it's a self-assessment. Use it to check yourself before leadership has to.

### Green — Thriving

You're on track. Activity metrics are hit or beaten. Pipeline is healthy and current. CRM is clean. You're closing at a pace consistent with hitting quota. You bring real contributions to meetings and implement coaching without being asked twice.

This is what the role looks like when it's working.

### Yellow — At Risk

Something is off. Maybe it's one area — activity is down, CRM is getting stale, deals have stalled. You're not in crisis, but the trajectory isn't right. Yellow is a warning that a conversation is coming if the trend continues.

If you're in yellow and you know it, don't wait to be told. Fix the thing and tell leadership what you're doing about it.

### Red — Termination Track

Multiple things are failing simultaneously, the trend line is down, and coaching hasn't produced visible change. This is not a static state — it's a trajectory. You can pull out of red, but it requires a sharp, visible change in behavior in a short window of time.

If you're in red and you don't know it, you're not reading the room.

## 30/60/90-Day Checkpoints

Accountability expectations are not uniform across the ramp period. Here's what each checkpoint looks like and what we're evaluating.

### Day 30 — Foundation Check

**What we're looking at:** Have you internalized the product, the process, and the rhythm?

- All onboarding sections completed or actively in progress
- CRM set up with real prospects, not placeholders
- Attending meetings prepared and participating
- Activity metrics being tracked daily (even if volume is still building)
- Completing at least 1–2 demos per week

**What we're not asking for yet:** Closed deals. You're learning the ramp. What matters here is that the inputs are in place.

### Day 60 — Ramp Check

**What we're looking at:** Are you in the game?

- Activity minimums hit consistently for the past 2–3 weeks
- Pipeline has 5+ legitimate opportunities with clear next steps
- At least 1 closed deal (or a clear near-close in progress)
- CRM is current and accurate — no coaching required on this
- Feedback from meetings is being implemented visibly

**What we're watching:** If activity is still inconsistent and pipeline is thin at day 60, the ramp protection conversation changes.

### Day 90 — Production Check

**What we're looking at:** Are you on a path to a real production number?

- Closing at or near quota pace
- Self-sourcing a meaningful portion of your own pipeline
- No persistent issues with CRM, meeting prep, or follow-up speed
- Demonstrating product knowledge independently on calls
- Pipeline is healthy enough to sustain next month's numbers

**What happens at day 90 if you're not here:** An honest conversation about trajectory. Not a surprise — you'll have had check-ins at 30 and 60. If you're not on track and you've been getting feedback, day 90 is the point at which the decision about the relationship gets made.

## The Bottom Line

You're in a commission-only role. That means two things: the upside is real, and the accountability is real. Nobody is going to micromanage you into success — but nobody is going to quietly ignore a rep who is consistently falling short of the basics either.

The reps who win here know what they need to do, do it without being asked, and bring back results. The reps who don't make it usually know they're struggling before leadership does — and they wait too long to say something.

If something is off, say something early. The window to fix it is always bigger than it looks from inside the problem.
