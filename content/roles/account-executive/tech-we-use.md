## Claude (Anthropic) — Your AI Force Multiplier

Claude is woven into how Obsidion operates. As an AE, you should be using Claude constantly to think sharper, move faster, and do more than any single rep could on their own.

### How AEs use Claude

- **Prospect research** — drop a company URL or LinkedIn profile into Claude and get a full breakdown: what they do, how big they are, what their pain points likely are, and what angle to lead with.
- **Outreach drafting** — generate personalized cold emails, LinkedIn messages, and follow-up sequences tailored to the prospect's industry and situation.
- **Objection prep** — before a call, feed Claude what you know about the prospect and get back likely objections with suggested responses.
- **Vertical brainstorming** — use Claude to explore new industries, niches, and angles you haven't considered. Ask it to identify underserved markets, seasonal opportunities, or emerging verticals.
- **Proposal writing** — draft custom proposal sections, ROI projections, and executive summaries.
- **Post-call analysis** — paste your call notes and have Claude extract action items, flag missed opportunities, and suggest next steps.

### Claude tools you should know

| Tool | What it does | When to use it |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Claude.ai** (web/desktop) | Conversational AI for research, drafting, and analysis | Daily — prospect research, email drafts, brainstorming |
| **Claude Co Work** | Collaborative AI workspace for longer projects | Proposal building, campaign planning, deep research |
| **Claude Code** | Terminal-based coding assistant | If you're building automations or custom outreach tools |

### Skill development

Reps who learn to prompt Claude well will consistently outperform reps who don't. Treat it like a junior analyst who's available 24/7 — the better your questions, the better the output. Experiment with it. Push it. The goal is to multiply your capacity so one AE can do the work of three.

## Notion — CRM & Command Center

Notion is the source of truth at Obsidion. Our custom dashboard houses the CRM and most of the operational data you'll interact with daily.

### What lives in Notion

- **Lead & contact database** — every prospect, every account, every interaction. This is where leads live and where pipeline is tracked.
- **Deal pipeline** — stages from first contact through close and handoff to delivery. Your commission-eligible revenue is tracked here.
- **Activity logging** — calls, emails, demos, proposals. If it's not in Notion, it didn't happen.
- **Client milestones** — post-sale project tracking (website builds, SEO onboarding, etc.) lives in the same workspace, different views.
- **Notes & handoff docs** — when you close a deal, the delivery team picks up from your notes in Notion.

### What you need to know

You don't need to be a Notion power user on day one. You need to know how to:
1. Find and claim inbound leads
2. Log calls and notes against a contact
3. Move deals through pipeline stages
4. Update deal values and close dates accurately
5. Pull your own pipeline report for team meetings

Your workspace and databases will be assigned during onboarding. If something is missing or broken, open a thread with your manager — don't work around the system.

## Loom — Video Sales Letters (VSLs)

Loom is one of the most powerful outbound tools in a modern sales rep's stack, and we want every AE using it aggressively.

### Why Loom matters

A personalized video in a prospect's inbox stands out. While every other agency is sending templated emails, you're sending a 60-second video where you've pulled up their website, walked through what's broken, and shown them exactly what you'd fix. That's the play.

### How to use Loom for outbound

- **Prospecting VSLs** — record short (60–90 second) personalized videos for cold outreach. Pull up their website, Google listing, or social media and narrate what you see and what you'd improve.
- **Follow-up videos** — after a demo or proposal, send a Loom recapping the key points and next steps. It keeps momentum and shows effort.
- **Proposal walkthroughs** — instead of just emailing a PDF, record yourself walking through the proposal. Explain the pricing, the deliverables, and why it's structured the way it is.
- **Re-engagement** — for stale deals, a quick personal video can restart a conversation that a text email never would.

### Best practices

- Keep videos under 2 minutes for cold outreach
- Always show their actual business (website, Google profile, social pages) — generic videos get ignored
- Use a clean background, good lighting, and look professional
- Include a clear CTA at the end ("I'll follow up Thursday — or grab 15 minutes here")
- Track opens and views in Loom to prioritize follow-ups on engaged prospects

## Apollo.io — Lead Generation & Prospecting

Apollo is your self-service lead engine. Don't wait for marketing to hand you leads — go find them.

### What Apollo does

- Search and filter prospects by industry, location, company size, revenue, tech stack, and more
- Pull verified email addresses and phone numbers
- Build targeted prospect lists for outreach campaigns
- Research companies before calls (funding, team size, tech used)

### Where to prospect

Apollo integrates with and pulls data from:
- **LinkedIn** — the primary B2B prospecting channel. Search by title, company, industry.
- **Company websites** — Apollo enriches company data with website info, tech stack, and more.
- **X (Twitter)** — useful for finding engaged business owners and decision-makers in certain verticals.
- **Facebook** — relevant for local business owners (restaurants, salons, contractors) who are active on FB business pages.

### How AEs should use it

1. Build lists by vertical and geography matching your territory or target market
2. Enrich contacts with verified emails and direct dials
3. Export into your outreach sequence (email + Loom + phone)
4. Use Apollo's intent data to prioritize prospects who are actively looking for solutions
5. Refresh lists weekly — don't work stale data

## Miro — Quick Presentations & Visual Pitches

Miro is a free collaborative whiteboard tool that's perfect for creating quick, visual presentations without needing a full slide deck.

### When to use Miro

- **Discovery call visuals** — map out a prospect's current state vs. where you'll take them
- **Quick proposals** — for smaller deals or early-stage conversations where a full PDF proposal is overkill
- **Process diagrams** — show prospects the onboarding flow, the build timeline, or how our products integrate
- **Competitive comparisons** — visual side-by-side of Obsidion vs. their current solution or alternatives

### Why it works

Sometimes a prospect needs to *see* the plan, not just hear it. Miro lets you throw something together in 10 minutes that looks polished and collaborative. Share the board link and walk through it live on a call, or send it async.

## Sales Call Recording — Accountability & Coaching

Every sales call at Obsidion is recorded, logged, and reviewable. This isn't optional.

### Why we record calls

- **Coaching** — leadership reviews calls to give specific, actionable feedback. This is how you get better fast.
- **KPI verification** — call recordings confirm activity metrics. If the numbers say 20 calls but the recordings show 8, that's a problem.
- **Quality assurance** — we can verify that prospects are being represented to honestly and that the product is being positioned correctly.
- **Training library** — great calls become training material for the team. Your best calls can be the example others learn from.

### What's tracked

- Call recordings (audio/video)
- Call duration and timestamps
- Participant details
- Call notes and outcomes
- Follow-up actions logged

### Your responsibility

- Ensure recording is enabled before every call
- Log call outcomes and notes in Notion immediately after
- Flag any calls where you need coaching or review
- Review your own calls weekly to self-assess

> The recording system is currently being finalized. Your team lead will confirm the specific tool and setup during onboarding. The expectation that all calls are recorded and logged is non-negotiable regardless of which tool is used.
