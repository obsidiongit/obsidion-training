# Remarketing

Most small businesses have the same problem: a lead comes in, nobody follows up fast enough, and it goes cold. Not because the owner doesn't care — because they're busy running the business. The lead filled out a form, called and got voicemail, or browsed the site and left. And nothing happened after that.

Remarketing is the automated follow-up system that catches those leads and works them until they either convert or opt out — without the owner having to do it manually every time.

---

## What It Does

**Automated email sequences**
Triggered by visitor behavior — form submissions, link clicks, appointment no-shows, or time-based re-engagement. Sequences run on their own once configured. The owner doesn't have to remember to follow up; the system does it.

**SMS follow-up**
For leads who opt in, SMS gets added to the sequence at key points. Response rates on SMS are significantly higher than email — when someone doesn't reply to an email, a text often gets the response. Opt-in compliance is handled in the setup.

**Lead nurture workflows**
For prospects who aren't ready to buy now, nurture sequences keep the business in front of them over time — relevant content, check-ins, seasonal offers — until the timing is right.

**CRM integration**
Every touchpoint is logged. The sequence triggers based on CRM status changes (new lead, contacted, no response, closed) and handoff points are tracked so nothing falls through. When a lead responds, the sequence pauses and routes to the owner.

---

## Why It Matters

The follow-up window is short. Research consistently shows that leads contacted within five minutes of submitting a form are dramatically more likely to convert than leads contacted an hour later. Most small businesses contact leads hours later — or not at all.

The pitch question that opens this up:

> *"If a lead fills out a form on your site tonight at 9pm, what happens — who sees it, and how fast do they follow up?"*

Most owners will either describe a manual process, admit it's inconsistent, or go quiet. That's the gap. Follow up:

> *"That's the problem we solve. The system picks it up the moment it comes in — email goes out in seconds, SMS follows if they don't respond, and you get a notification to jump in when they engage. The leads don't go cold waiting on a manual follow-up."*

## Workflow Sequences

These are the core sequences. Each can be adjusted for vertical, tone, and trigger logic — but these are the starting points.

---

### New Lead (Form Fill / Web Inquiry)

**Trigger:** Contact form submitted, quote request, or inquiry landing page conversion.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Message type |
|------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Immediate | Email | Acknowledgment — "Got your message, we'll be in touch shortly" |
| 2 | Immediate | CRM alert | Owner/AE notification to call now |
| 3 | Day 1 (if no response) | Email | Follow-up — restate what they asked about, prompt next step |
| 4 | Day 3 | SMS | Short, direct — "Hey [Name], still looking into [service]? Happy to help." |
| 5 | Day 7 | Email | Closing the loop — "Didn't hear back — still interested or not the right time?" |
| 6 | Day 10 | SMS | Final touch before moving to nurture |
| 7 | Day 14+ | Nurture sequence | Long-form re-engagement (see below) |

**Rule:** As soon as a lead responds at any step, the automated sequence pauses and routes to the owner. The sequence is the safety net — the owner still closes the deal.

---

### Missed Call

**Trigger:** Inbound call goes unanswered during or after business hours.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Message type |
|------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Immediate (within 60 sec) | SMS | "Hey, saw you called — what can I help you with?" |
| 2 | Day 1 (if no reply) | Email | Follow-up — "Tried to reach you, still happy to connect" |
| 3 | Day 3 | SMS | Second attempt, different angle |
| 4 | Day 7 | Email | Final follow-up before moving to nurture |

**Note:** The immediate SMS after a missed call is one of the highest-converting touches in the system. Most businesses lose these leads entirely. This catches them at the moment they're still thinking about calling.

---

### Post-Purchase / Post-Service

**Trigger:** Transaction completed, service delivered, or appointment marked complete in CRM.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Message type |
|------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Email | Thank you + review request (link to Google or platform of choice) |
| 2 | Day 7 | Email | Check-in — "How did everything go?" |
| 3 | Day 30 | Email or SMS | Re-engagement offer — loyalty reward, referral ask, seasonal promotion |
| 4 | Day 60 | Email | Soft upsell or rebooking prompt |

**Note:** The review request at Day 1 is time-sensitive — that's when the experience is freshest. Businesses that automate this step consistently outpace competitors on Google review count.

---

### Re-Engagement (Lapsed Customer)

**Trigger:** No activity (purchase, booking, or open) within a defined window — typically 30, 60, or 90 days depending on the industry.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Message type |
|------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 0 (trigger fires) | Email | "We miss you" — acknowledge the gap, offer a reason to come back |
| 2 | Day 7 (if no open) | SMS | Shorter version of the same message |
| 3 | Day 14 | Email | Final outreach — limited-time offer or direct ask |
| 4 | Day 21 (no response) | System | Move to suppressed list — stop sending, protect sender reputation |

**Note:** Suppression at the end matters. Continuing to email unengaged contacts hurts deliverability across the whole list. The sequence has a defined end.

## Ad Retargeting

Ad retargeting is a separate layer from the email/SMS sequences — it's not triggered by a form fill or a call. It's triggered by a site visit.

**How it works:** A pixel placed on the client's website tracks visitors. When a visitor leaves without converting, they start seeing the client's ads across Google Display, Facebook, and Instagram. The ads follow them — a reminder that the business exists.

**When to pitch it:** Retargeting is most valuable when the client already has meaningful web traffic — either from SEO or paid search. Without traffic, there's nothing to retarget. Lead with email/SMS for low-traffic businesses; introduce retargeting once the traffic foundation is there.

**How to explain it on a call:**

> *"You know how you look at something online and then you start seeing ads for it everywhere? That's retargeting. We set that up for your business. Someone visits your site, doesn't book or fill out a form, and then for the next 30 days your brand follows them around the internet. Most of your competitors aren't doing this for a business your size."*

**Retargeting vs. email/SMS:** These work differently and aren't substitutes for each other. Email and SMS reach people who've already given their contact info — they're warmer. Retargeting reaches anonymous visitors who browsed and left — it works on volume and visibility. Both together close more of the funnel.

## Common Client Questions

**"We already send emails — how is this different?"**
> "Sending a newsletter is broadcast marketing — you're pushing the same message to everyone at the same time. Behavioral sequences are triggered by what a specific person did: they filled out a form, they called and missed, they haven't come back in 60 days. The message is relevant to where they are, not just what you want to promote today. That's why the conversion rates are higher."

**"Do I need to write all the content myself?"**
> "No — we build the sequences and write the copy as part of the setup. You review and approve, but you're not starting from a blank page. Once it's live, it runs without you writing anything new unless you want to."

**"What if customers opt out of SMS?"**
> "Opt-outs are handled automatically — anyone who replies STOP gets removed from SMS sequences immediately and won't be sent to again. Compliance is built into the system. We set it up correctly from day one so there's no manual management required."

**"How does it know when to trigger?"**
> "It's connected to your CRM and website. When someone fills out a form, the CRM creates the lead and the sequence fires. When a call comes in and goes unanswered, the missed call trigger fires. When a transaction closes, the post-purchase sequence starts. The triggers are defined at setup — you don't have to flip any switches day-to-day."

**"What's a realistic conversion rate on these sequences?"**
> "Depends on the industry, the offer, and how warm the lead was to begin with. The more honest answer is: it's almost always better than zero follow-up, which is what most businesses are working with now. The missed call SMS alone — going out within 60 seconds — recovers leads that would have just called the next person on the list."

**"Does this replace my CRM, or work with it?"**
> "Works with it. The sequences run through your Obsidion CRM — if you're already using something else, we look at what integrations are available before committing to an answer. The goal isn't to add more tools; it's to make the follow-up automatic regardless of what's already in place."
