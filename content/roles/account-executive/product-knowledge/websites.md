# Custom Websites

The diagnostic question that opens this product:

> *"If someone found your business on Google right now and landed on your website — would you be comfortable with that?"*

Most business owners pause. They know the answer. The site is outdated, it loads slow on mobile, or it was built by someone's nephew five years ago and nobody's touched it since. Some don't even have a site — just a Google Business Profile or a Facebook page. The website is the foundation of everything else Obsidion builds on top of, and if it's broken, slow, or missing, nothing else performs the way it should.

---

## What It Does

**Custom design — not templates**
Every site is built from scratch for the client's industry, not adapted from a generic template. The design reflects their brand, addresses their specific customer's decision-making process, and is built to convert — not just look good in a screenshot.

**Mobile-first, performance-optimized**
More than half of local business searches happen on a phone. Sites are built mobile-first, with fast load times, clean layouts, and CTA placement designed for a thumb, not a cursor. Core Web Vitals scores are factored into the build, not patched on after.

**Conversion-focused structure**
Every page has a job. The homepage establishes trust and routes the visitor. Service pages handle objections and push toward contact. The build isn't just visual — it's structured around how a local customer evaluates and chooses a business.

**Compliance-conscious design**
For regulated industries — cannabis, automotive, healthcare-adjacent — the site is built with compliance considerations baked in. This includes age gates, required disclosures, and content structures that don't trip platform ad policies.

**Analytics and conversion tracking**
Google Analytics, call tracking, and form submission tracking are configured at launch. The client knows how many people are landing on the site, where they're coming from, and whether they're converting — from day one.

## What Clients Typically Have Before

**DIY Wix or Squarespace**
The owner built it themselves, or a family member did. It functions, but it looks like it. Slow load times, template design that doesn't differentiate them from competitors, no integrations, and no tracking set up. Often hasn't been updated since the day it launched.

**Outdated WordPress**
An agency built it four to six years ago. Hasn't been touched since. Plugins are out of date, mobile experience is poor, and the original agency either doesn't exist anymore or charges a high hourly rate for changes. The owner doesn't have access to the backend or doesn't know how to use it.

**No site at all**
The business runs off a Google Business Profile and a Facebook page. They have no owned web presence — no place to send people, no conversion surface, no SEO foundation. Everything lives on platforms they don't control.

The framing for all three:

> *"Whatever you have right now — whether it's outdated, DIY, or nothing — we're building the thing you can actually be proud to send people to. Custom, fast, integrated with your systems, live in seven days."*

## The 7-Day Delivery Process

Seven days from kickoff to live site. Here's what happens inside that window:

**Day 1 — Kickoff**
The onboarding questionnaire is reviewed and a kickoff call happens with the build team. Brand assets are collected (logo, photos, any existing copy worth keeping). Scope is locked — pages, features, integrations, and compliance requirements. The client knows exactly what they're getting before anything is built.

**Days 2–3 — Build**
Design and development. Pages are built, content is written and populated, and integrations are configured. The Obsidion Dashboard is connected — booking, contacts, calendar, and invoicing set up to sync with the site. If a POS integration is in scope, that gets configured against the client's existing system (or a new one is selected and set up).

**Days 4–5 — Review**
A preview link goes to the client. They review on desktop and mobile. One revision round is included — changes are scoped and addressed. This is also when call tracking numbers are assigned and analytics is wired up.

**Day 6 — Pre-Launch QA**
Final internal review. Forms tested, integrations confirmed, load speed benchmarked, mobile layout checked across device sizes. Any final client revisions from Day 5 are resolved.

**Day 7 — Launch**
DNS is updated, the site goes live, hosting is confirmed, and the client gets access to their dashboard. Post-launch, a brief walkthrough happens so they know how to update basic content without calling us every time.

## Integrations

**Obsidion Dashboard**
All sites are integrated with the Obsidion Dashboard by default — the client's central hub for booking management, contact records, calendar, and invoicing. This connects the site to their operations: form submissions become CRM entries, booking widgets sync with the calendar, and invoicing is accessible from one place.

**POS & Third-Party Systems**
POS integration depends on what the client already has. If they're running Square, Toast, Clover, or another system, we assess what's available and connect where possible. If they don't have a POS or want to consolidate, we work with them to find the right fit for their operation before the build starts.

> *"If they have a system they're already using, we figure out whether we can integrate it. If they don't have one, or the one they have isn't working well, we're not going to force them into something that doesn't fit — we'll look at what makes sense for the business."*

## Common Client Questions

**"Why not just use Wix or Squarespace ourselves?"**
> "You can — and a lot of our clients tried that first. The honest difference: template builders are built for ease, not performance or conversion. The sites come out slow, they look like templates because they are, and there's no integration with your booking system or CRM. We build something purpose-built for your industry. It also takes about ten hours of your time to do it poorly — we do it in seven days."

**"What do you need from us to get started?"**
> "An onboarding questionnaire that takes about ten minutes, your logo and any brand assets you have, and one kickoff call. If you have an existing site with content worth keeping, share the link. That's it on your end for the build phase."

**"What happens if we need changes after launch?"**
> "Basic content updates — hours, services, pricing — you can handle yourself through your dashboard. Structural changes, new pages, or anything more involved comes through us. How that's scoped depends on the tier you're on."

**"Do we own the site?**
> "Yes. The site is yours. We host it as part of your plan, but the asset belongs to you — if you ever moved away from Obsidion, you'd take it with you."

**"Can you redesign an existing site or just build new?"**
> "Both. If there's existing content worth keeping — copy, photography, service descriptions — we bring it into the new build. If the current site is too far gone, we start fresh and make it faster. Either way, the output is purpose-built, not a patch job on top of what you had."

**"What about the domain?"**
> "If you already have a domain, we connect to it. If you don't have one yet, we help you get one. Domain registration is separate from the build — we'll walk through that at kickoff so it doesn't hold up the launch."
