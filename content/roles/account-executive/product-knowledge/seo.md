# SEO Services

Most small business owners assume they show up on Google because they have a website. They don't check. They don't know what page they're on, whether their Google Business Profile is optimized, or whether their competitors are outranking them for every relevant search in their market. That's the gap.

The diagnostic question that opens this conversation:

> *"When did you last Google your business category and city? Not your name — your category. Like '[their industry] in [their city].' Do you know where you show up?"*

Most won't know. Some will pull it up on the call and find out live. Either way, that's the moment the pitch earns itself.

## What It Does

**Google Business Profile (GBP) optimization and management**
GBP is the single highest-leverage local SEO asset most businesses either haven't claimed or haven't properly built out. Photos, service categories, hours, Q&A, review responses, posting cadence — all of it affects how Google ranks the listing in map results. We optimize it at setup and manage it ongoing.

**On-page SEO**
Title tags, meta descriptions, header structure, keyword targeting, internal linking, and content alignment. The goal is making sure every page on the site tells Google exactly what it's about and for whom — and that the information matches what local searchers are looking for.

**Technical SEO**
Site speed, mobile performance, schema markup, crawlability, Core Web Vitals. These are the under-the-hood factors that affect how Google reads and ranks the site. Most small business sites have technical issues that quietly suppress rankings — this clears them.

**Local citation building and consistency**
Citations are mentions of the business name, address, and phone number (NAP) across directories — Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing, industry-specific sites. Inconsistency across these signals hurts local rankings. We audit existing citations, clean up inconsistencies, and build new ones in relevant directories.

**Review management strategy**
Volume and recency of Google reviews directly affects local pack rankings. We set up a system for consistently requesting reviews from customers and provide guidance on response strategy — both for positive reviews and negative ones.

**Monthly reporting and rank tracking**
Keyword positions, GBP metrics, organic traffic, and call volume tracked monthly. The client can see what's moving and why — not just a PDF of numbers, but context on what the data means.

## What to Expect — Timeline

SEO is not a fast channel. Anyone who tells a prospect otherwise is setting them up to churn. Be direct about the timeline — it builds credibility and sets the relationship up correctly.

**Month 1 — Setup**
GBP is optimized and fully built out. On-page fixes go live. Citation audit runs, inconsistencies get corrected, new citations start going out. Technical issues are identified and resolved. Rank tracking baseline is established. The client may not see movement yet — this month is infrastructure.

**Months 2–3 — Foundation Settles**
Google re-indexes updated pages. Citations stabilize across directories. GBP engagement typically starts to tick up — more calls, more direction requests, more clicks. Some movement on lower-competition keywords. This is the period where the work is clearly happening but the results aren't dramatic yet.

**Months 3–4 — First Measurable Movement**
Rank improvements start showing on target terms, especially for less competitive local searches. GBP call volume up meaningfully from baseline. Organic traffic starting to move. This is usually the point where a skeptical client becomes a believer.

**Month 6+ — Compounding Results**
The channel compounds. Rankings on primary terms strengthen, content gains authority, the GBP listing becomes a consistent lead source. This is where the investment pays back — and unlike paid ads, the results don't disappear the day the spend stops.

The honest framing for a prospect:

> *"Month one is setup, month three is when you start seeing movement, month six is when you look back and realize the channel is working. If someone's promising you page one in 30 days, they're either lying or doing something that'll get your site penalized."*

## DIY vs. Done-For-You

Plenty of owners have heard of Semrush or Moz or Ahrefs. Some have even logged in. The distinction to make clearly:

> *"Those tools will show you exactly what's wrong with your SEO. They won't fix it. The work is rewriting 40 title tags, cleaning up citations across 30 directories, fixing schema errors, publishing optimized content, and doing it consistently every month. That's what we do. Tools give you data; we do the work."*

The real competition isn't DIY tools — it's owners who looked at the data, felt overwhelmed, closed the browser, and did nothing. Obsidion removes the execution barrier.

## Common Client Questions

**"How long until I see results?"**
> "Month one is setup and infrastructure. You'll start to see movement around month three — GBP engagement, rank improvements on lower-competition terms. Month six is when the channel is clearly working. I'd rather set that expectation now than have you evaluate us after 30 days."

**"Can't I just do this myself?"**
> "You can learn it — there's good information out there. The honest question is whether you have 10–15 hours a month to execute it consistently while running your business. Most owners who try end up doing the research, not the work. We handle the execution so you don't have to."

**"My nephew / a freelancer offered to do this cheaper."**
> "Maybe worth asking what's included — specifically. GBP management, citation cleanup, on-page optimization, technical fixes, monthly rank reporting? Those are the things that move the needle. If the price is much lower, something's usually missing or it's one-time setup with no ongoing work. SEO isn't a one-time fix; it's a monthly operation."

**"We already show up on Google when I search our name — why do we need this?"**
> "Searching your name is branded search — people who already know you exist. What we're optimizing for is category search: someone in your market who's never heard of you, searching for what you do. That's the traffic you're either capturing or missing. Want to check right now?"

**"What's the difference between SEO and paid ads?"**
> "Paid ads buy you placement — the moment you stop paying, the traffic stops. SEO earns placement over time — once you're ranking, you're getting that traffic for free. Both have a role, but SEO compounds. Ads don't."

**"How do I know it's working?"**
> "Monthly reports — keyword positions, GBP call volume, organic traffic, direction requests. We show you the baseline at setup so every month is a comparison against where you started, not just a number without context."
