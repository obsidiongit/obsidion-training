# Mobile App

Most businesses rent access to their customers through someone else's platform — Instagram, DoorDash, Yelp, whatever third-party app their customers happen to use. The Obsidion mobile app changes that. It's a fully branded iOS and Android app that lives on their customer's phone under their name, with their logo, in the App Store. That's the lead.

---

## What It Does

**Custom branded app (iOS & Android)**
The app is built and published under the client's brand — their name in the App Store, their colors, their logo. Not a white-label template that looks generic. Their customers see their business, not a platform.

**Push notifications**
Direct line to the customer's lock screen. Email open rates hover around 20%. Push notification open rates run 60–90%. When the client wants to promote a flash sale, announce a new product, or fill slow-day capacity, push is the fastest channel they have.

**Loyalty & rewards program**
Points, punch cards, spend-based tiers — configurable to how the business actually works. Loyalty data lives in the app, not in a third-party system the client doesn't own. Customers track their rewards in real time, which drives repeat visits.

**Online ordering & booking**
Integrated ordering or appointment booking without routing customers through a third-party platform that takes a cut or redirects them to a competitor. The transaction stays inside their ecosystem.

**Customer accounts**
Order history, saved preferences, loyalty balance, notification settings — all in one place. Reduces friction on repeat purchases and gives the client a cleaner picture of who their best customers are.

**App analytics**
Usage data, push open rates, loyalty redemptions, order volume by channel — visible in the client's dashboard. This is what turns the app from a feature into a system.

## Ideal Client Profile

The app is strongest when two factors overlap: **repeat customers** and **transaction volume**. The more of both, the easier the sell.

**Green light — lead with the app:**
- Restaurants, cafés, or food & bev businesses with regulars
- Dispensaries with loyalty-driven clientele and compliance needs around direct outreach
- Salons, med spas, or fitness studios with recurring appointments and retention goals
- Any business already running a loyalty program through a clunky third-party tool

**Yellow light — sellable, but build the case first:**
- Business has repeat customers but low transaction frequency (e.g., quarterly visits) — loyalty value is weaker, but push notifications may still be the hook
- Business has high volume but mostly new customers — the app can still drive referrals and retention, but frame it as a growth tool, not a retention tool
- Owner is skeptical customers will download it — address this directly (see Common Questions below)

**Red light — deprioritize or don't lead with it:**
- One-time or very low-frequency purchase businesses (e.g., contractors selling single projects)
- Very small customer base — not enough volume to justify the channel
- Client is unwilling to actively promote the app to their customers post-launch

## How to Pitch It

Lead with the branded presence angle. The question that opens it up:

> *"Right now, when your customers order from you or book an appointment — are they doing that through your system or through someone else's app?"*

If they're using a third-party platform (or nothing at all), that's the gap. Follow up:

> *"The problem with third-party platforms is your customer is technically on their app, not yours. You're renting access to people who already know you. We put you in the App Store under your own name — your brand, your loyalty program, your push notifications. That's a channel you own."*

Then anchor on the push notification stat if they're skeptical about ROI:

> *"Most businesses email promotions and get 20% open rates if they're lucky. Push notifications to people who downloaded your app run north of 60%. If you're trying to fill a slow Tuesday or move inventory, that's the difference."*

## Common Client Questions

**"Do customers actually download apps for local businesses?"**
> "Depends on how much the business promotes it and what's in it for the customer. If there's a loyalty reward tied to downloading — a free item, a discount on the first order — adoption rates are strong. Businesses that hand a card at checkout saying 'download our app, get X' see results. The app doesn't sell itself; it's a tool the owner has to activate."

**"How is this different from just posting on Instagram or sending emails?"**
> "Social media is rented reach — the algorithm decides who sees it. Email is better but still gets filtered and ignored. Push notifications go directly to the lock screen of people who specifically opted in by downloading your app. That's a different relationship. These are your most loyal customers, and you have a direct line to them."

**"What does it take to get this live?"**
> "We handle the build and App Store submission. From the client's side, we need branding assets, their menu or service list if it's relevant, and decisions on how they want the loyalty program structured. Timeline depends on complexity, but it's not a months-long project."

**"What if I already use Square, Toast, Mindbody, or another platform?"**
> "We integrate with most major POS and booking platforms, so this isn't necessarily a rip-and-replace conversation. The app becomes the customer-facing layer — the place customers go to order, book, or check their rewards — while the backend tools they already use can stay in place. Worth a closer look at their specific setup before committing to that answer."

**"Who manages the app after it's live?"**
> "Day-to-day, the client controls push notifications and promotions through their dashboard — it's designed to be manageable without a tech team. Updates, maintenance, and any technical issues run through Obsidion. They're not on their own."

**"What if customers don't engage with it after they download it?"**
> "That's usually a push strategy problem, not an app problem. If they're not sending notifications, running loyalty promotions, or giving customers a reason to open it, engagement drops — same as any channel. We help set up the first few campaigns so there's a playbook to follow, not just a live app and a 'good luck.'"
