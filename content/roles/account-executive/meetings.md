# Meeting Cadence & Preparation

---

## Meetings Are Not Optional

Every day at 10:00 AM Eastern, the team gets on a call. It's on Discord. Leadership runs it. If you're an active AE, you're on the call — no exceptions unless you've communicated in advance.

This is not a status update you can Slack in. This is where the team aligns on pipeline, shares what's working, surfaces blockers, and hears directly from leadership. The reps who treat these meetings as a priority are the ones who stay calibrated. The ones who skip them drift — and drifting in a commission-only role means starving quietly.

---

## Meeting Format

### Schedule

Daily at 10:00 AM Eastern. Put it in your calendar and protect it like a client demo.

### Platform

**Discord voice/video.** Be in the channel on time. Camera is encouraged but not required. Mic is required — you're expected to participate, not spectate.

### Who Runs It

Founder / company leadership. This is top-down alignment, not a roundtable brainstorm. You'll have the floor when it's your turn.

### Duration

Tight, structured, time-boxed. If it goes long, it's because something important surfaced — not because people are rambling.

---

## Meeting Structure

Every meeting follows the same format. Know it. Prepare for it.

| Segment | Time | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening** | 0–2 min | Leadership sets the agenda. Any company updates, product news, or announcements. |
| **Pipeline Round-Robin** | 2–18 min | Each AE gives a quick update: top deals, expected closes this week, specific blockers or asks. |
| **Wins & Lessons** | 18–23 min | What closed since last meeting? What fell through and why? One win, one lesson — keep it tight. |
| **Coaching / Focus Topic** | 23–28 min | Leadership covers one focus area: objection handling, talk track refinement, product update, process change. |
| **Close** | 28–30 min | Action items, reminders, next meeting confirmation. |

## What You Bring to Every Meeting

Show up prepared or don't show up. Coming to a pipeline meeting without your numbers is like showing up to a demo without knowing the product — it wastes everyone's time, including yours.

### Pre-Meeting Checklist

Before the meeting starts, have these ready:

- **CRM is current.** Every deal has an accurate stage, a next step, and recent notes. If your CRM is stale, update it before the meeting — not during.
- **Your top 3 deals.** Know them cold: who, what stage, what's the blocker, what's the next action, when is it expected to close.
- **Your numbers.** Dials this week, demos run, proposals sent, deals closed. Know them without looking them up.
- **One specific ask.** If you need help — a talk track for a specific objection, a second opinion on pricing, a warm intro — bring it. These meetings are the fastest way to get answers.
- **One win or one lesson.** Something that worked, or something that didn't and what you learned. The team gets better when people share real intel, not just "things are going well."

### What Not to Bring

- Excuses. If the numbers are bad, own it and bring a plan to fix it.
- Vague updates. "Working some deals" is not a pipeline update. Names, stages, next steps.
- Questions you could have Slacked. Save the meeting for things that benefit from live conversation.

## Attendance Policy

### Expectations

- Attendance is mandatory unless you've communicated to leadership ahead of time.
- "Ahead of time" means before the meeting, not during or after.
- If a client call conflicts, that takes priority — but tell your manager and get a recap.

### What Happens If You Miss

- **First absence without notice:** Noted. You'll hear about it in your next 1:1. Not the end of the world, but it's on the record.
- **Repeat absences:** Treated as a performance issue. If you're consistently missing meetings, it signals disengagement — and in a commission-only role, disengagement is the first sign that things aren't working.

There is no formal punishment system. But leadership notices. Your teammates notice. And in a team this size, being the person who doesn't show up is a reputation you don't want.

---

## Why This Matters

In a remote, commission-only environment, these meetings are one of the few places where the team exists as a team. They prevent isolation. They keep standards visible. They give you a rhythm beyond your own head.

The best AEs use these meetings to sharpen their approach. They listen to what other reps are running into, steal talk tracks that are working, and get coaching on deals they're stuck on. The worst AEs treat them as an interruption to their day. The difference shows up in the numbers within a month.

Show up. Be prepared. Participate. Every day, 10:00 AM Eastern.
