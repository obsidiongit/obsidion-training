# Performance Expectations

---

## Let's Get Something Straight

This is a commission-only position. There is no base salary. There is no draw. There is no safety net. You eat what you kill — period. If that makes your stomach drop, this isn't the role for you, and there's zero shame in that. But if you're still reading, understand that the reps who thrive here are the ones who treat the phone like a weapon and outwork everyone in the room.

This is feast or famine. The comp plan is built to reward volume and consistency — not talent alone, not charm alone, and definitely not "I'll get to it tomorrow." The numbers below aren't suggestions. They're the baseline for survival. Exceed them and you'll make serious money. Fall short and you'll feel it in your bank account before anyone has to say a word.

---

## The Reality of Commission-Only

Your income is determined by exactly two things:

1. **Front-end commissions** — 40% of every setup fee you close. A standard $1,000 setup puts $400 in your pocket. An enterprise $10,000 setup puts $4,000 in your pocket. No cap.
2. **MRR bonus tiers** — Every active customer's monthly fee stacks toward your MRR total. Hit the thresholds, unlock escalating monthly bonuses that compound over time.

There is no third thing. Nobody is coming to save you with a base salary adjustment. Your pipeline is your paycheck.

### What the Math Looks Like

| Scenario | Deals / Month | Annual Commissions | Annual MRR Bonus | Total Annual Comp |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Barely Surviving** | 4–6 | ~$24,000 | ~$3,600 | ~$27,600 |
| **Ramp-Up** | 8 | $38,400 | $16,800 | $55,200 |
| **Solid** | 15 | $72,000 | $43,200 | $115,200 |
| **Crushing It** | 20 | $96,000 | $64,800 | $160,800 |
| **Elite** | 20+ with enterprise | $144,000+ | $108,000+ | $252,000+ |

Look at "Barely Surviving." That's what 4–6 deals a month gets you — less than $30K a year with no benefits safety net. That's not a career, that's a warning sign. Now look at "Crushing It" and "Elite." That's what happens when you commit to the activity. The gap between broke and $250K+ is not talent — it's dial volume and consistency.

## Daily Activity — Non-Negotiable Minimums

This is an outbound, high-volume sales role. If you're doing 35 dials a day, you are going to starve. That is not an exaggeration. The connect rates in this space mean you need massive dial volume to generate enough live conversations to fill a pipeline that actually pays your bills.

### The Dial Targets

| Activity Level | Dials / Day | What It Means |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Minimum expectation** | 150 | This is the floor. Below this, you are not doing the job. |
| **Competitive** | 200–250 | This is where consistent closers operate. |
| **Elite** | 300+ | The reps making $200K+ are here. Every single one of them. |

**150 dials per day is not a stretch goal — it is the minimum standard to remain in this seat.**

### How the Funnel Works

Here's what 150–300 dials per day actually produces:

| Metric | At 150 Dials/Day | At 250 Dials/Day | At 300 Dials/Day |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Live conversations** (15–20% connect) | 22–30 | 37–50 | 45–60 |
| **Qualified discoveries / day** | 4–6 | 7–10 | 9–12 |
| **Demos booked / week** | 8–12 | 15–22 | 20–28 |
| **Deals closed / month** (30–40% close rate) | 8–12 | 15–22 | 20–28 |

See the pattern? The rep doing 300 dials is closing 2–3x what the rep doing 150 dials closes. Same product, same market, same comp plan. The difference is entirely effort.

### Daily Activity Scorecard

Track these every single day. No exceptions.

| Metric | Minimum | Competitive | Elite |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Outbound dials** | 150 | 200–250 | 300+ |
| **Live conversations** | 22 | 37 | 45+ |
| **Qualified discoveries** | 4 | 7 | 9+ |
| **Demos / deep dives booked** | 2 | 3–4 | 5+ |
| **Follow-up touches sent** | 10 | 15 | 20+ |

If your CRM isn't updated by end of day, it didn't happen. Every conversation gets logged. Every demo gets a next step. Every follow-up gets scheduled. This is how you build a pipeline that compounds instead of one that leaks.

## Ramp Expectations — 30 / 60 / 90 Days

There is no "ramp protection." There is no reduced quota for your first few months. You are commission-only from day one. That said, here's what a realistic ramp looks like if you're putting in the work:

### Days 1–30: Learn the Playbook, Start Dialing

- Complete the full training playbook and product knowledge modules
- Shadow live calls in the first week — you should be on the phone yourself by day 3–5
- **Dial target from week 2 onward: 100–150/day** (building to full volume)
- Goal: Run your first qualified discoveries and book your first demos
- You should be able to walk a prospect through the full value story without slides by day 14
- **Realistic closes this month: 2–6** (you're learning, but you're still dialing)

### Days 31–60: Pipeline Has Real Depth

- Full dial volume: **150–200+ dials/day**, no excuses
- Pipeline should have 15–25 active opportunities at various stages
- Weekly cadence is locked in: discovery, demos, follow-ups, closes
- CRM is clean — every deal has a stage, a next step, and notes
- **Realistic closes this month: 6–12**
- You should be approaching or clearing Tier 1 MRR ($3,000/month)

### Days 61–90: Full Production or Serious Conversation

- Full activity benchmarks: **200+ dials/day**
- Closing 8+ deals/month consistently
- Pipeline is self-sustaining — new opportunities replacing closed ones weekly
- MRR should be tracking toward Tier 2 ($5,000/month)
- **If you are not at minimum benchmarks by day 90, we need to have a direct conversation about fit**

This is not meant to scare you. It's meant to respect your time. If the volume isn't there by month three, this role is going to cause you more pain than reward — and both sides deserve honesty about that.

## Review Cadence

### Weekly: Pipeline Review (1:1 with Manager)

- Bring your pipeline view and your numbers — dials, conversations, demos, closes
- Bring 2–3 call recordings for coaching (wins and losses)
- This is where you get better. Not a gotcha session — a tuning session
- If your dials are below minimum, expect this to be the first topic

### Monthly: Performance Scorecard

- Full month activity vs. benchmarks
- Revenue generated (commissions + MRR progress)
- Pipeline health: new opportunities in vs. deals closed out
- Trajectory check: are you on pace for the next MRR tier?

### Quarterly: Formal Review

- Total revenue impact and comp earned
- MRR tier status and trajectory
- Territory and vertical performance breakdown
- Honest assessment: what's working, what's not, what changes

## What Separates the Reps Who Make It

**They dial.** That's it. That's the secret. The reps making $150K–$250K+ in this role are not smarter than you. They are not more talented than you. They pick up the phone more than you. They do it when they don't feel like it. They do it when the last five calls went nowhere. They do it first thing in the morning and they do it at 4:45 PM.

Beyond raw volume, the habits that compound:

- **Speed to follow-up** — Same-day recap emails with the questionnaire link. Day 3 value-add touch. Day 7 call with a new angle. The follow-up cadence is in your call framework docs — use it
- **Honest forecasting** — Your pipeline should reflect reality, not optimism. If a deal is dead, kill it. A clean pipeline is a productive pipeline
- **Multi-threading** — When the deal matters, don't hang your commission on one contact. Get above and beside your champion
- **Clean handoffs** — When a deal closes, delivery gets everything they need. No ambiguity, no chasing you for details. This protects your MRR because happy customers don't churn
- **Log everything** — If it's not in Notion, it didn't happen. Calls, next steps, objections, competitor intel — all of it

## If You're Falling Behind

Don't wait for someone to tap you on the shoulder. If your numbers are off pace:

1. **Check your dials first.** Nine times out of ten, the problem is volume, not skill
2. **Bring it to your 1:1.** Your manager can help with list quality, talk tracks, and territory strategy — but only if you surface the issue early
3. **Listen to your own calls.** The recordings exist for a reason. Compare your calls to the top performers
4. **Audit your follow-up.** Deals don't die on the first call — they die in the follow-up gap

The reps who wash out of commission-only roles almost always share the same trait: they waited too long to ask for help because they thought the numbers would magically improve. They won't. Dial volume and coaching fix pipelines. Hope doesn't.
