## Overview

Nobody expects you to show up on day one already running at full ramp. These milestones describe how competence and pipeline momentum typically build — your manager will calibrate territory-specific quotas and KPIs to match your market.

## How Your Rhythm Builds

### Week 1 — Immersion and First Reps

You are deep in the playbook, shadowing live calls, and putting real touches on the board. Notes might feel rough — that is expected. The win is protecting calendar time for outreach and treating every conversation as practice, not a verdict on your future here.

### Week 4 — Things Start to Click

The stack feels less abstract: your CRM tells a coherent story, follow-ups get easier to schedule, and you can walk a prospect through the offer without leaning on slides. You will notice the difference in how calm you feel going into the next call.

### Month 3 — Momentum You Can Feel

Pipeline has real depth — several active opportunities, stages that match reality, and a weekly cadence that lines up with the benchmarks your plan defines. This is what sustainable production feels like: repeatable motion, fewer heroic saves, and honest forecasting.

## The Longer Arc: 30 / 60 / 90 Days

### First 30 Days

Aim for fluency: finish the playbook, shadow several live calls, and run your first batch of qualified conversations. The goal is to explain the full value story in your own words.

### Days 31–60

Your pipeline becomes the system you trust — stages, next steps, and notes on every active deal. Week over week, you want a steady mix of discovery and follow-ups on the calendar.

### Days 61–90

You are operating at or toward full activity benchmarks and closing eligible deals (or hitting the ramp milestone your plan spells out). Early feedback loops matter more than waiting to be "fully ready."

## Activity Benchmarks (Examples)

These numbers illustrate where experienced reps often land at full ramp — your targets will reflect ACV, territory, and channel mix.

| Metric                       | Target           | Note                          |
|-----------------------------|------------------|-------------------------------|
| Quality touches / day        | 35–50            | Calls, meaningful DMs, booked meetings |
| Demos or deep dives / week   | 6–10             | After qualification           |
| Closes / month               | 2–4              | At full ramp; varies by ACV   |

## Habits That Compound

**What great looks like over time**: Fast, specific follow-up; forecasting that matches reality; multi-threaded deals when it matters; and handoffs to delivery that leave no ambiguity. These are skills you grow into — not a bar you are expected to clear on week one.

**Where your manager helps**: Pace, list quality, and talk tracks all improve faster with coaching. Bring your pipeline view and a few call snippets to one-on-ones; the goal is to tune the system together, not to catch you off guard at quarter-end.

## If Something Feels Off

Speak up early. Whether it is list quality, product clarity, or calendar load, the fastest fixes happen when you surface friction in the first weeks — not when targets are due.
