# Getting Paid & Bonuses

## Account Executive Compensation & Bonus Program

---

## Program Overview

This compensation plan rewards Account Executives for both new business acquisition and long-term account retention. The program consists of two primary components:

- **Front-End Commission:** A one-time commission on the initial setup fee for each new account closed.
- **MRR Milestone Bonuses:** Ongoing monthly bonus payments tied to the total Monthly Recurring Revenue managed in your active book of business.

The goal is to align incentives so that AEs are motivated to close new business, close larger deals, and retain customers month over month.

---

## Front-End Commission

Earned on every new account closed, paid out on the billing cycle following the customer's first payment.

### Commission Rate

- **40% of the Initial Setup Fee**
- Applies to ALL deal sizes equally

### Example Calculations

| Deal Type          | Setup Fee | AE Commission (40%) |
| ------------------ | --------- | ------------------- |
| Standard Account   | $1,000    | $400                |
| Mid-Tier Account   | $5,000    | $2,000              |
| Enterprise Account | $10,000   | $4,000              |

### Payment Terms

- Commission is paid on the pay cycle following the customer's first successful payment.
- If a customer cancels within the first 30 days and receives a refund, the commission is clawed back.
- Commission applies to the setup fee only — the monthly recurring amount is handled through the MRR Bonus Tiers.

## MRR Bonus Tier System

Your monthly bonus is determined by the total Monthly Recurring Revenue across all active accounts you manage. As your book of business grows, you unlock higher bonus tiers — and receive that bonus check every single month.

### How It Works

1. Every active customer in your book of business contributes their monthly fee toward your total MRR.
2. When your total MRR reaches a tier threshold, you unlock that tier's monthly bonus.
3. You receive this bonus as a monthly check — every month you maintain the MRR at or above the threshold.
4. If your MRR drops below a tier due to churn, your bonus adjusts down to the qualifying tier.

## Enterprise & High-Value Deal Treatment

Larger deals follow the same commission and bonus structure — they simply accelerate tier progression.

- **Front-End Commission:** 40% of the setup fee, just like standard deals. A $10,000 setup = $4,000 commission.
- **MRR Contribution:** The customer's full monthly rate (whether $500, $1,000, or $3,000/month) counts toward your total MRR and your bonus tier qualification.
- No separate enterprise commission tier — the system naturally rewards larger deals through faster tier progression and higher MRR accumulation.

### Enterprise Deal Scenario

| Account Type            | Count | Monthly Rate | MRR Contribution |
| ----------------------- | ----- | ------------ | ---------------- |
| Standard ($297/mo)      | 20    | $297         | $5,940           |

This AE qualifies for Tier 3 ($1,200/month bonus) with just 25 accounts and $11,440 in MRR, and is well on their way to Tier 4 thanks to enterprise accounts accelerating their MRR.

## Rules & Policies

### Account Ownership

- You own the accounts you close. The monthly revenue from those accounts counts toward your MRR as long as they remain active.
- If an account is transferred to another AE (for any reason), the MRR transfers with it.
- Shared or co-sold deals will have MRR split as agreed in writing prior to the close.

### Churn & Tier Maintenance

- Your bonus tier is evaluated on the 1st of each month based on your total active MRR.
- If customer cancellations cause your MRR to drop below your current tier threshold, your bonus adjusts down to the qualifying tier.
- There is a **1-month grace period**: if your MRR dips below a tier threshold for a single month, you retain your current tier bonus that month. If it remains below the threshold for a second consecutive month, you drop to the lower tier.
- This incentivizes retention and proactive account management, not just acquisition.

### Commission Clawback

- If a customer cancels or disputes the setup fee within 30 days of activation and receives a refund, the 40% front-end commission will be deducted from the AE's next pay cycle.
- No clawback applies after the 30-day mark.

### Bonus Payment Schedule

- MRR milestone bonuses are paid monthly, typically on the 15th of the following month (e.g., January's bonus is paid February 15th).
- Front-end commissions are paid on the standard pay cycle following the customer's first successful payment.

## FAQ

**When do I get my first commission check?**
On the pay cycle following the customer's first successful payment. Commissions don't pay on verbal agreements — the customer must be billed and paid.

**What if a customer cancels in the first month?**
If they cancel within 30 days and receive a refund, the front-end commission is clawed back from your next pay cycle. After the 30-day mark, no clawback applies.

**What happens if my MRR drops below a tier?**
You get a 1-month grace period. If your MRR stays below the threshold for a second consecutive month, you drop to the qualifying tier. This incentivizes proactive account management and retention.

**Do enterprise deals have a different commission rate?**
No. The commission rate is 40% of the setup fee regardless of deal size. Enterprise deals are rewarded through larger setup fees and faster MRR tier progression.

**How are co-sold deals handled?**
MRR is split as agreed in writing prior to the close. Get the split documented before the deal closes.

**What's the draw structure?**
Base draw/salary details are confirmed in your offer letter. This compensation program covers commission and bonus income on top of your base.
