## Call Flow — Quick Reference

### 1. Intro & Permission (0–2 min)

Confirm time, name the agenda, ask if now still works. Don't push through if it's a bad moment — reschedule immediately.

> *"Quick agenda: I want to hear how you're currently handling [website / local search / follow-up], share how we typically fix that for businesses like yours, and see if it makes sense to go deeper. If it doesn't fit, I'll tell you straight. Sound good?"*

---

### 2. Discovery Before Pitch (2–15 min)

Map their current setup — site, SEO, lead follow-up, referrals. One question at a time. Quantify pain where you can. The goal is to get them to say something out loud they haven't said to themselves yet.

> *"Walk me through what happens when someone finds you online today — do you know where you show up?"*

---

### 3. Tailored Pitch (15–25 min)

Don't pitch features. Pitch what they told you. Tie each product area to a specific discovery finding. One story beats five feature bullets. Pause every 90 seconds — you want them steering, not just listening.

> *"Based on what you said — especially [specific pain they named] — here's how we typically solve that. I'll keep it tight, you steer if I'm off."*

---

### 4. Handle Objections Live

Label it, validate it, reframe it. Sound human, not scripted. Every objection is a signal the prospect is still engaged — silence is worse.

> *"That makes sense — a lot of businesses we work with had the same concern before we fixed [X]. What specifically went wrong before?"*

---

### 5. Close or Next Step (25–30 min)

Propose a specific next step tied to a specific date. Never end on "I'll follow up sometime." The move is the Quick Sites questionnaire — frame it as scoping, not paperwork.

> *"The way we get started is a quick onboarding questionnaire — takes about ten minutes, lets our team scope exactly what you need before we send anything over. I'll drop the link right after this call. Does that work?"*

---

## The Non-Negotiables

- **Discovery before pitch.** Every time. You can't tailor a pitch from zero information.
- **One story beats five features.** Pick the result that matches their vertical and lead with it.
- **Never end without a next step.** A specific action tied to a specific date. "I'll follow up" is not a close.
- **Log everything.** Status, last contact, next action — in Notion after every call.
