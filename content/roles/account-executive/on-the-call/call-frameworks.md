# Call Frameworks

---

## Discovery Call Framework (30 min)

The goal of a first call isn't to close — it's to earn the next step. Specifically: get them interested enough to fill out the Account Creation questionnaire on the Quick Sites page. That's the handoff. Build toward it.

### Structure

| Phase | Time | What you're doing |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Intro & permission** | 0–2 min | Confirm time, name the agenda, ask if now still works |
| **2. Situation** | 2–10 min | Current setup, tools, pain points — listen more than you talk |
| **3. Impact** | 10–15 min | What that pain is actually costing them — make it real |
| **4. Solution alignment** | 15–25 min | Map Obsidion's stack to what they told you |
| **5. Next step** | 25–30 min | Get them to the Quick Sites questionnaire |

---

### Phase 1 — Intro & Permission (0–2 min)

Don't assume they remember why you're calling. Name it fast.

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making time — I know 30 minutes is real time. Quick agenda: I want to hear how you're currently handling [website / local search / lead follow-up], share how we typically fix that stack for businesses like yours, and see if it makes sense to go deeper. If it doesn't fit, I'll tell you straight. Sound good?"

If they say now isn't great — **don't push through it.** Reschedule immediately and hold that time firm. A distracted prospect is a closed door.

---

### Phase 2 — Situation (2–10 min)

You're mapping their current setup. Think of it like a diagnostic, not an interrogation. One question at a time, then shut up and listen.

**Core questions:**

- "Walk me through what happens when someone finds you online today — do you know where you show up?"
- "When a lead comes in after hours or fills out a form, what's the process — who picks it up and how fast?"
- "Are you on any kind of marketing retainer right now, or handling it internally?"
- "How long have things been set up the way they are?"
- "What's pushed you to think about this now — something change recently?"

The last question is critical. If there's urgency, name it back to them later.

---

### Phase 3 — Impact (10–15 min)

This is where most AEs rush. Don't. The impact conversation is what makes a prospect feel the problem — and people buy to solve problems they feel, not ones they've acknowledged intellectually.

Your job here is to quantify the pain where you can:

- "So if you're not showing up on maps or organic, how many calls a week do you think you're missing?"
- "If a lead goes cold because no one followed up fast enough, what does one client represent in revenue?"
- "You mentioned your site is outdated — are you sending people there in your outreach anyway? What happens when they land on it?"

You don't need exact numbers. You need them to say something out loud that they haven't said to themselves yet. That's the moment the call turns.

---

### Phase 4 — Solution Alignment (15–25 min)

Don't pitch features. Pitch what they told you.

Open the pitch by echoing their words back:

> "Based on what you said — especially [specific pain they named] — here's how we typically solve that. Three layers: the site as the hub, local visibility so people find you first, and automated follow-up so leads don't go cold between touchpoints. I'll keep it tight, you steer if I'm off."

Then walk through the relevant product areas — only the ones that map to their pain. If they didn't mention mobile apps, don't bring them up.

**One story beats five features.** Pick one specific result relevant to their vertical and lead with it.

---

### Phase 5 — Next Step (25–30 min)

The goal is one thing: get them to fill out the Account Creation / Onboarding questionnaire on the [Quick Sites page](https://obsidion.com/quick-sites) of the website. That's the handoff that moves them into the pipeline.

**Trial close:**

> "If we could solve [their #1 pain], is that worth spending ten minutes on a questionnaire so we can scope the right fit for you?"

**Directing them to the questionnaire:**

> "The way we get started is through a quick onboarding questionnaire — it takes about ten minutes and lets our team scope exactly what you need before we send anything over. I'll drop the link in our follow-up email right after this call. Does that work?"

If they're warm but not quite there:

> "No pressure to fill it out tonight — but I'll send it over and we can set a time to walk through what comes back together. What's your week looking like Thursday?"

**Never end a call without a specific next action tied to a specific date.** "I'll follow up sometime" is not a next step — it's a slow death.

## Qualifying Signals

Listen for these during Phase 2 and 3. They tell you whether to go deeper or redirect.

**Green lights — signs of strong fit:**

- Active pain they can describe in their own words (bad site, lost leads, no SEO presence)
- Recent trigger (new competition, opened a second location, just parted ways with an agency)
- Decision-maker on the call, or clearly identified
- They're already spending on marketing and not seeing results

**Yellow lights — proceed but probe:**

- "We've been meaning to deal with this for a while" — no urgency yet, dig for the trigger
- "My [partner / owner / manager] handles that" — clarify who needs to be in the next conversation
- "We tried something similar before" — ask what went wrong specifically before positioning

**Red lights — disqualify or deprioritize:**

- No budget and no timeline ("maybe next quarter" with no specifics)
- Not the decision-maker and unable to loop one in
- Locked in a long-term contract with no exit near
- Looking for something Obsidion doesn't do (e.g., paid ad management only)

## Closing Framework

### Trial Close Language

Use these before proposing the questionnaire to test the temperature:

> "If we could get your site live in under two weeks and start pulling in local search traffic — is that the kind of result that would justify the investment?"

> "It sounds like lead follow-up is the biggest leak right now. If we automated that, what would change?"

> "Is there anything that would make this a no before we even look at scope?"

### The Questionnaire Handoff

This is the close for a first call. It's low-friction and moves the process forward without requiring a decision on the spot.

1. Summarize the 2–3 pain points they named
2. Connect each to what Obsidion solves
3. Frame the questionnaire as a way to get them a scoped, relevant proposal — not a generic pitch deck

> "Rather than send you a brochure, our process starts with a quick intake form so our team can build something specific to your situation. It's on the Quick Sites page — I'll send the link in my follow-up email right after this. Takes about ten minutes and then we'll have a real starting point."

### Handling "I Need to Think About It"

Don't let this become a stall. Address it before it lands by testing for objections during the impact phase. If you still get it at the end:

> "Totally fair. What's the part you want to think through — is it timing, fit, or something about how we work? I'd rather address it now than have you sit on a question I can answer in two minutes."

If they're genuinely not ready, get the questionnaire in front of them anyway and book a follow-up to review it together. A warm prospect with a task in front of them is still in motion.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Track all touches in Notion. Every prospect should have a clear status, last contact date, and next action logged.

| Touch | Timing | Channel | What you're doing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Day 0 (same day as call) | Email | Send the Quick Sites questionnaire link + summary of what you talked about |
| **2** | Day 3 | Email | Value-add follow-up — a result, a resource, or a relevant angle. Not "just checking in." |
| **3** | Day 7 | Call | Second attempt with a new angle or a response to something they said on call 1 |
| **4** | Day 10 | Email | Reference the questionnaire specifically — "Takes 10 minutes, gets you a real proposal" |
| **5** | Day 14 | Call or DM | Final proactive touch before moving to nurture sequence |

**Day 0 email template:**

> Subject: Quick follow-up — [their business name]
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> Good talking through [the thing they mentioned]. Based on what you shared, I think there's a real fit — especially around [their #1 pain].
>
> Next step is the Quick Sites questionnaire: [link]. It takes about 10 minutes and lets our team scope what makes sense for your situation before we put anything in front of you.
>
> Happy to walk through it together if that's easier — just let me know.
>
> [Your name]

## Second Call: Bringing in a Decision-Maker

If the first call ends with "I need to loop in my [partner / owner / manager]":

1. Get the DM's name and role on the call before you hang up
2. Offer to draft a short summary they can forward — this keeps the momentum without them having to do the work
3. Book the second call with a firm time before leaving the first one

> "What's a time this week or next that works for both of you? I'll send a calendar invite so it's on both your radars."

On the second call, don't restart from zero. Briefly recap what the first conversation surfaced, then open it up:

> "I brought [first contact's name] up to speed, but I'd love to hear it from your side — what does the current [website / lead process / marketing] look like from your perspective?"
