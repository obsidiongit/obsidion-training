# Call Scripts

These are starting points, not lines to read verbatim. The goal is to sound like a person — use these to internalize the structure and the logic, then say it in your own voice.

---

## Opening Talk Tracks

### Warm Lead

> "Thanks for making the time. I'd love to understand what's working with how you attract and retain customers today, and where it feels brittle — website, Google, or follow-up after someone shows interest. I'll share how we usually fix that stack for businesses like yours, and you can tell me if it's relevant."

---

### Cold Lead

Lead with getting them talking. If they push back, pivot to booking a time.

**First attempt — get them talking now:**

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion — we build websites, local search, and lead follow-up systems for [their industry] businesses. Quick question: when someone Googles [their category] in [their city], do you know where you're showing up? I ask because that's usually where we find the gap. One minute?"

If they engage, move into discovery. If they push back:

**Fallback — book the call:**

> "Totally fair — I know I'm calling cold. I'm not trying to pitch you right now, just figure out if there's a fit worth a real conversation. Is there a 20-minute window this week I could get on the calendar? I'll come prepared with what I already know about your market so we're not wasting time."

---

## Discovery Prompts

One question at a time. Ask, then stop talking. The silence does the work.

**Online presence & visibility**
- "When someone searches for what you do in your city, where do you show up — maps, organic, both, neither?"
- "Have you Googled yourself recently? What did you see?"
- "Are you on the first page for any local search terms, or is that something you honestly don't know yet?"

**Lead flow & follow-up**
- "What happens to a lead who calls after hours or DMs you — who touches it, and how fast?"
- "If a lead fills out a form on your site today, what's the actual process — where does it go, who picks it up?"
- "How many leads do you think go cold just because nobody followed up fast enough? Ballpark."

**Capacity & growth**
- "If we 10x'd web leads tomorrow, could your team handle the volume without chaos?"
- "Is growth the goal right now, or is the focus more on tightening up what you already have?"

**Current marketing & vendors**
- "Are you on any kind of marketing retainer right now, or handling it in-house?"
- "Have you worked with an agency or vendor before? What happened?"
- "What's pushed you to think about this now — something change recently?"

**Competition & market**
- "Are you seeing more competition in your area lately, or has your market stayed pretty stable?"
- "When a customer chooses a competitor over you, do you usually know why?"

**Referrals**
- "Right now, where do most of your new customers come from — word of mouth, Google, social, something else?"
- "Is referral business consistent, or does it come in waves?"

## Transition to Offer

Use this after discovery — restate their pain before you pitch anything.

> "Based on what you said — especially [repeat their specific pain] — here's how we typically solve that in three layers: the site as the hub, local visibility so people find you first, and automated follow-up so leads don't go cold between touchpoints. I'll keep it tight and you steer if I'm off."

If they named multiple pain points, anchor on the one with the most urgency:

> "You mentioned a few things, but the one that stood out to me was [specific pain]. That's exactly the kind of problem we built our system around — let me walk you through how we usually fix it."

## Voicemail Script

Keep it under 30 seconds. Don't pitch. Give them one reason to call back.

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your name] with Obsidion — [Your number]. I was calling because we work with a lot of [their industry] businesses in [their market] on websites, local search, and follow-up — and I had a specific question about how you're set up. Give me a call back when you get a chance: [Your number]. Again, that's [Your name] at [Your number]."

Read your number at normal pace. Repeat it once. That's the whole job of a voicemail.

---

## Follow-Up Email — No Answer / No Voicemail Pickup

Short. One hook. One ask.

> **Subject: Quick question — [their business name]**
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> Tried reaching you earlier — no big deal. One question I was going to ask: when someone Googles [their category] in [their city], do you know where you're showing up?
>
> If it's not a fit, I'll leave you alone. If it's worth 20 minutes, I'm easy to reach: [your number].
>
> [Your name]

## Gatekeeper Navigation

### When you know the owner's name

> "Hey, is [Owner's name] around? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."

If asked what it's regarding:

> "I work with [their industry] businesses on their website and local search — I had a quick question for [Owner's name] specifically. Is he / she available?"

Don't oversell to the gatekeeper. Keep it short and confident.

---

### When you don't know who to ask for

> "Hey, quick question — who handles the marketing or website side of things there? I just want to make sure I'm talking to the right person."

Once you have a name:

> "Perfect — is [Name] available? It's [Your name] from Obsidion."

If they ask what it's about:

> "We work with [their industry] businesses on websites and local search. I had a specific question for them — takes about two minutes."

Don't pitch the gatekeeper. Get a name, get a transfer, or get a callback time.

---

## Referral Ask

Use this with clients who are actively happy — after a win, a compliment, or a strong check-in. Don't rush it.

> "Really glad to hear it's working. Quick question — is there anyone else in your network running a business like yours who you think could use the same kind of system? I'm not asking you to sell them on anything, just a name and a heads up that I might reach out."

If they hesitate:

> "No pressure at all — I just find the best clients come from people who already know what we do. If anyone comes to mind, even a few weeks from now, I'd appreciate the intro."

Log referral names in Notion the same day. Follow up on every one.
