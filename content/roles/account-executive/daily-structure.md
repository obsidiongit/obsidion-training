# Daily Structure

---

## Your Day Is Not Optional

This is a commission-only role. Nobody is watching you clock in. Nobody is going to remind you to make your calls. The structure below exists because the reps who make real money in this seat all run some version of it — and the reps who flame out share one thing in common: they wing it.

You own your schedule. That's the upside of this role. The downside is that if you waste it, you feel it in your bank account — not in a performance review three months from now. There is no buffer between effort and income.

The blocks below are a recommended framework, not a rigid rulebook. Adapt the timing to your life. But every block needs to happen, every day, in some form. Skip one and the pipeline starts leaking. Skip two and the pipeline dries up.

---

## Recommended Daily Time Blocks

This is what a productive day looks like. The specific hours are flexible — shift them to fit your schedule. What's not flexible is the sequence: launch, attack, close out.

### Morning Launch (first 60–90 min)

This is not email and coffee. This is the most important 90 minutes of your day.

| Block | What you're doing | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline review | Open your CRM. Rank every active deal by close probability. Know your top 3 by name before you pick up the phone. | You can't push deals forward if you don't know where they stand. |
| Inbound check | Check for overnight leads — web forms, questionnaire submissions, voicemails. Claim and respond within SLA. | Speed to lead wins. The rep who responds in 10 minutes closes at 4x the rate of the one who waits an hour. |
| Calendar prep | Confirm every demo and call scheduled today. Pull up notes, prior conversations, pricing — know the deal cold before the call starts. | Walking into a demo unprepared is how you lose deals you already earned. |
| Daily 3 | Write down 3 non-negotiable outcomes for the day. Not tasks — outcomes. "Book 2 new demos" not "make calls." | Forces clarity. At the end of the day, you either hit them or you didn't. No ambiguity. |

### Outbound Attack (next 2–3 hours)

The first outbound block is sacred. This is dial time. No Slack, no admin, no "I'll start after lunch." The reps making $200K+ in this role protect this block like their paycheck depends on it — because it does.

- Cold and warm outreach: calls, emails, LinkedIn
- Target: 75–150 dials in this block alone
- Log every conversation in the CRM immediately — not "later"
- If you're not getting connects, check your list quality before blaming the market

### Midday Execution (core hours)

This is where scheduled calls, demos, and active deal work happen.

| Activity | Standard |
|---|---|
| Run demos and discovery calls | As scheduled — never reschedule without a strong reason |
| Post-call CRM updates | Immediately after hanging up. Notes, next steps, stage changes. Not at end of day. |
| Follow-up on proposals | Nudge, handle objections, push to close. Proposals sitting untouched for 3+ days are dying. |
| Pipeline check-in | Midday gut check — are you on pace for daily activity targets? If not, adjust now, not tomorrow. |

### Second Outbound Block (if call volume is light)

If your demo calendar has gaps, fill them with dials. An AE with a light afternoon and a phone in their hand is an AE building next week's pipeline. An AE browsing Slack is an AE who's going to wonder why next month's commission check is thin.

### End of Day Close-Out (final 30–45 min)

| Task | Non-negotiable? |
|---|---|
| Update every active deal in CRM | Yes. No stale records overnight. If it's not current, you're lying to yourself about your pipeline. |
| Log all activity metrics | Yes. Calls, emails, demos, proposals. This is how you prove the work happened. |
| Prep tomorrow's priority list and calendar | Yes. Walking in tomorrow without a plan means you'll spend the first hour figuring out what to do instead of doing it. |
| Flag at-risk deals or blockers | Yes. If something is stuck, leadership can only help if they know about it. |
| Submit daily activity report | Yes. Format will be defined by your team lead. |

---

## The Point

Your hours are flexible. Your output is not. Whether you start at 7 AM or 10 AM, the blocks above need to happen. The daily checklist on this page tracks the individual tasks — use it every day. Reset it every morning. Build the habit.

The reps who treat this like a real job — with real structure and real discipline — are the ones cashing real checks. The ones who "get to it when they feel like it" are the ones updating their LinkedIn three months from now.
